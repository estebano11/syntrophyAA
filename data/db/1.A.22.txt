<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='center'><strong>1.A.22  The Large Conductance Mechanosensitive Ion Channel (MscL) Family</strong></p>
<p>MscL of <em>E. coli</em> has been extensively characterized, and limited functional studies have been performed on some of its homologues (<span id='ref112.7543101' class='cite'>H&auml;se <em>et al</em>., 1995</span>; <span id='ref115.8809939' class='cite'>Sukharev et al., 1996</span>, <span id='ref121.10352145' class='cite'>1999</span>; <span id='ref122.10501827' class='cite'>Sukharev <em>et al</em>., 1999</span>, <span id='ref123.11217861' class='cite'>2001</span>). The MscL protein of <em>E. coli</em> is 136 amino acyl residues in length and spans the membrane twice as &alpha;-helices (<span id='ref113.8890153' class='cite'>Blount <em>et al</em>., 1996a</span>,<span id='ref114.8876191' class='cite'>b</span>). It forms a homopentameric channel with ten TMSs (<span id='ref113.8890153' class='cite'>Blount <em>et al</em>., 1996a</span>,<span id='ref114.8876191' class='cite'>b</span>; <span id='ref122.10501827' class='cite'>Sukharev <em>et al</em>., 1999</span>). The channel transports ions fairly nonspecifically with slight selectivity for cations over anions (<span id='ref111.7511799' class='cite'>Sukharev <em>et al</em>., 1994</span>). Mechanosensitivity has been demonstrated for several MscL homologues using patch-clamp methodology (<span id='ref113.8890153' class='cite'>Blount <em>et al</em>., 1996a</span>,<span id='ref114.8876191' class='cite'>b</span>; <span id='ref116.9405414' class='cite'>Blount <em>et al</em>., 1997</span>; <span id='ref115.8809939' class='cite'>Sukharev <em>et al</em>., 1996</span>). It has been shown to release proteins such as thioredoxin during osmotic downshift (<span id='ref117.9756908' class='cite'>Ajouz <em>et al</em>., 1998</span>). Expression of the <em>E. coli</em> <em>mscL</em> gene has been shown to protect <em>Vibrio alginolyticus</em> and <em>Bacillus subtilis </em>from cell lysis during osmotic downshift (<span id='ref119.10050752' class='cite'>Nakamaru <em>et al</em>., 1999</span>; <span id='ref4960.18310427' class='cite'>Hoffmann <em>et al</em>., 2008</span>). Mutational loss of  the hydrophobic interaction between membrane lipids and the periplasmic rim of the channel's funnel impairs the function of MscL, presumably by blocking channel opening (<span id='ref34553328.15041651' class='cite'>Yoshimura <em>et al.</em> 2004</span>). Cyclodextrins can be used for structural and functional studies of mechanosensitive channels (<span id='ref34554085.34816118' class='cite'>Zhang <em>et al.</em> 2021</span>). Structural elements in water and ion permeation through an MscL have been identified using molecular dynamics simulation (<span id='ref34554910.35765322' class='cite'>Naeini <em>et al.</em> 2022</span>).&nbsp;<span id='ref34555100.35836929' class='cite'>Crea <em>et al.</em> 2022</span> used an azobenzene-derived lipid analogue to optically activate MscL. Such an  approach allows photoactivation and control of cellular processes as complex as gravitropism and turgor sensing in plants, contractility of the heart, and sensing pain, hearing, and touch in animals. A variety of structurally distinct agonists have been shown bind to MscL directly, close to a transmembrane pocket that has been shown to have an important role in channel mechanical gating (<span id='ref34556400.37021145' class='cite'>Lane and Pliotas 2023</span>).</p>
<p>A novel force transduction pathway from a tension sensor to the gate in the mechano-gating of an MscL channel has been considered (<span id='ref34556807.37347044' class='cite'>Sawada <em>et al.</em> 2023</span>).&nbsp; Mutation at the tension sensor (F78N) in TMS2 caused decreased interaction of this residue not only with lipids, but also with a group of amino acids (Ile32-Leu36-Ile40) in the neighboring TMS1 helix, which resulted in an inefficient force transmission to the gate-constituting amino acids in TMS1. This change also induced a slight tilting of TMS1 towards the membrane plane and decreased the size of the channel pore at the gate, which seems to be the major mechanism for the inhibition of spontaneous opening of the double mutant channel. The newly identified interaction between the TMS2 (Phe78) and adjacent TMS1 (Ile32-Leu36-Ile40) helices seems to be an essential force transmitting mechanism for the stretch-dependent activation of MscL, given that substitution of any one of these four amino acids with Asn resulted in severe loss-of-function (<span id='ref34556807.37347044' class='cite'>Sawada <em>et al.</em> 2023</span>).&nbsp; The transcriptomic profile of the mechanosensitive ion channelome in human cardiac fibroblasts revealed that (1) agrees with previously documented findings that all currently identified mechanosensitive channels play a significant and well recognized physiological function in elucidating the mechanosensitive characteristics of HCFs; (2) supports earlier preliminary reports that point to the most common expression of the TRP mechanosensitive family in HCFs; and (3) points to other new mechanosensitive channels (TRPC1, TRPC2, TWIK-2, TMEM16A, ASIC1, and ASIC3) (<span id='ref34557737.38158807' class='cite'>Mitrokhin <em>et al.</em> 2023</span>).</p>
<p>MscL is gated by changes in bilayer deformation and by the membrane potential (<span id='ref34545838.17996439' class='cite'>Andersson <em>et al.</em> 2008</span>). The structure of the MscL channel in membranes of varying thickness and curvature has been studied (<span id='ref34549227.30413241' class='cite'>Wang <em>et al.</em> 2018</span>). Temperature-sensitive mutants have revealed aspects of the thermodynamic stability of the MscL structure (<span id='ref34549837.31143940' class='cite'>Owada <em>et al.</em> 2019</span>).&nbsp; Membrane tension is not a mediator of long-range intracellular signaling, but local variations in tension mediate distinct processes in sub-cellular domains (<span id='ref34550237.30392960' class='cite'>Shi <em>et al.</em> 2018</span>). Allosteric activation of MscL channels is triggered by lipid-mediated modification of mechanosensitive nano-pockets. Single-channel recordings have revealed a significant decrease in the pressure activation threshold of the modified channel and a sub-conducting state in the absence of applied tension (<span id='ref34550378.31601809' class='cite'>Kapsalis <em>et al.</em> 2019</span>). Synergistic modes of regulation by lipid molecules in membrane tension-activated mechanosensitive MscL channels have been decribed and discussed (<span id='ref34554325.34986323' class='cite'>Wang <em>et al.</em> 2021</span>). Hybrid-supported lipid bilayers (HSLBs) contain phospholipids and diblock copolymers.&nbsp;<span id='ref34554395.35014398' class='cite'>Manzer <em>et al.</em> 2021</span> used cell-free expression of MscL to assemble it with HSLB by either cotranslational integration of the protein into hybrid vesicles, followed by fusion of these proteoliposomes, or by preformation of a HSLB followed by the cell-free synthesis of the protein directly into the HSLB.</p>
<p>The three-dimensional structure of the <em>M. tuberculosis</em> MscL has been solved to 3.5 &Aring; resolution, and the crystal structure has been shown to reflect that in the intact cell membrane (<span id='ref118.9856938' class='cite'>Chang <em>et al</em>., 1998</span>; <span id='ref6162.11479346' class='cite'>Perozo <em>et al</em>., 2001</span>). This structure provided the basis for a model that explains gate opening and closing in response to membrane tension. Tension is proposed to expand the 10 TMS/5 subunit transmembrane barrel via the linker between the two TMSs [S1 (N-terminal) and M1 (C-terminal)]. S1 segments form a bundle when the channel is closed, and cross-linking between S1 segments prevents opening. S1 and M1 interact in the open channel, and cross-linking S1 to M1 impedes channel closing.  The opening of MscL is accompanied by the disassociation of a carboxl-terminal protrusion and pore formation (<span id='ref4979.18310324' class='cite'>Yoshimura <em>et al</em>., 2008</span>). Phylogenetic, structural and functional analysis have been presented by Pivetti et al. (<span id='ref2368.12626684' class='cite'>2003</span>). How these channels may respond to change in the mechanical environment the lipid bilayer provides is discussed by <span id='ref7421.20825352' class='cite'>Kung <em>et al.</em> (2010)</span>.&nbsp; Channel opening uses a helix-tilt mechanism and opens to a 2.8 nm diameter pore (<span id='ref10944.24550255' class='cite'>Wang <em>et al.</em> 2014</span>). Water may act as a 'lubricant' (softener) during TM1 helix
elongation that may play a role in gating (<span id='ref34545995.27753526' class='cite'>Bavi <em>et al.</em> 2016</span>).</p>
<p>Price et al. (<span id='ref7430.21134371' class='cite'>2011</span>) have demonstrated in vitro synthesis and oligomerization of the mechanosensitive channel, MscL, into functional ion channels. They showed that insertion requires YidC (2.A.9.3.1) but subsequent oligomerization to the functional pentamer occurs spontaneously.&nbsp; MscL acts as an 'emergency relief
valve', protecting bacteria from lysis upon acute osmotic down-shock. MscL is
reversibly and directly gated by changes in membrane tension. In the open state, MscL forms a non-
selective 3 nS conductance channel which gates at tensions close to the lytic limit of the bacterial
membrane. An earlier crystal structure at 3.5 A resolution of a pentameric MscL from <em>Mycobacterium
tuberculosis</em> represented a closed-state or non-conducting conformation. MscL has a complex gating
behaviour; it exhibits several intermediates between the closed and open states, including one
putative non-conductive expanded state and at least three sub-conducting states.&nbsp; <span id='ref11009.19701184' class='cite'>Liu <em>et al.</em> 2009</span> presented the crystal structure of a carboxy-terminal truncation mutant
(Delta95-120) of MscL from <em>Staphylococcus aureus</em> (SaMscL(CDelta26)) at 3.8 A resolution.&nbsp;
SaMscL(CDelta26) forms a tetrameric channel with both transmembrane helices tilted away from the
membrane normal at angles close to that inferred for the open state, probably corresponding to a
non-conductive but partially expanded intermediate state (<span id='ref11009.19701184' class='cite'>Liu <em>et al.</em> 2009</span>).&nbsp;</p>
<p>The MscL channel functions as a last-ditch emergency release valve, discharging cytoplasmic solutes upon decreases in the osmotic pressure.&nbsp; Opening this large gated pore allows passage of&nbsp; molecules up to 30 &Aring; in diameter (<span id='ref34554916.35685356' class='cite'>Immadisetty <em>et al.</em> 2022</span>). MscL undergoes large conformational changes and&nbsp; contains structural/functional themes that recur in higher organisms and help elucidate how other, structurally more complex, channels function. These features of MscL include (i) the ability to directly sense and respond to biophysical changes in the membrane, (ii) an alpha helix ('slide helix') or series of charges ('knot in a rope') at the cytoplasmic membrane boundary to guide transmembrane movements, and (iii) important subunit interfaces that, when disrupted, appear to cause the channel to gate inappropriately (<span id='ref34554916.35685356' class='cite'>Immadisetty <em>et al.</em> 2022</span>). MscL may have medical applications: the modality of the MscL channel can be changed, suggesting its use as a triggered nanovalve in nanodevices, including those for drug targeting. The antibiotic streptomycin opens MscL and uses it as one of the primary paths to the cytoplasm.</p>
<p>Assembling transmembrane proteins on organic electronic materials is a promising approach to couple biological functions to electrical readouts. A biosensing device produced in such a way would enable both the monitoring and regulation of physiological processes and the development of new analytical tools to identify drug targets and new protein functionalities. Transmembrane proteins can be interfaced with bioelectronics through supported lipid bilayers (SLBs).&nbsp;<span id='ref34555889.36651574' class='cite'>Manzer <em>et al.</em> 2023</span> demonstrated that cell-free expression systems allow for the one-step integration of MscL into SLBs assembled on an organic conducting polymer, poly(3,4-ethylenedioxythiophene) polystyrenesulfonate (PEDOT:PSS).&nbsp; MscL adopts the correct orientation, remains mobile in the SLB, and is active on the polyelectrolyte surface using optical and electrical readouts (<span id='ref34555889.36651574' class='cite'>Manzer <em>et al.</em> 2023</span>).</p>
