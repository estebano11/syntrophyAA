<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>1.A.78 The K<sup>+</sup>-selective Channel in Endosomes and Lysosomes (KEL) Family&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p>Organelle K<sup>+</sup> conductance studies revealed a major K<sup>+</sup>-selective channel, KEL or TMEM175, on endosomes and
lysosomes (<span id='ref34544487.26317472' class='cite'>Cang <em>et al.</em> 2015</span>). Unlike any of the
approximately 80 plasma membrane K<sup>+</sup> channels in mammals, TMEM175 has two repeats of 6 TMSs and has no GYG K<sup>+</sup> channel sequence signature in a pore (P) loop. Lysosomes
lacking TMEM175 exhibit no K<sup>+</sup> conductance, have a markedly depolarized &Delta;<em>psi</em> and little
sensitivity to changes in [K<sup>+</sup>]. These mutants have compromised luminal pH stability and abnormal fusion with
autophagosomes during autophagy. Thus, TMEM175 comprises a K<sup>+</sup> channel that underlies the molecular
mechanism of lysosomal K<sup>+</sup> permeability (<span id='ref34544487.26317472' class='cite'>Cang <em>et al.</em> 2015</span>).&nbsp;<span id='ref34557025.37524211' class='cite'>Wu <em>et al.</em> 2023</span> have reviewed the research progress on the structural, modulatory, and functional properties of TMEM175.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>TMEM175 establishes a membrane potential essential for lysosomal function, and its dysregulation is associated with the development of Parkinson's Disease. It is evolutionarily distinct from all other known channels, predicting novel ion-selectivity and gating mechanisms.&nbsp;<span id='ref34551246.32228865' class='cite'>Oh <em>et al.</em> 2020</span> presented cryo-EM structures of human KEL in open and closed conformations at resolutions of up to 2.6 &Aring;. It adopts a homodimeric architecture with a central ion-conduction pore lined by the side chains of the pore-lining helices. Conserved isoleucine residues in the center of the pore serve as the gate in the closed conformation. In the widened channel in the open conformation, these same residues establish a constriction essential for K<sup>+</sup> selectivity (<span id='ref34551246.32228865' class='cite'>Oh <em>et al.</em> 2020</span>). 4-Aminopyridine inhibits the lysosomal TMEM175 channel (<span id='ref34555497.36279431' class='cite'>Oh <em>et al.</em> 2022</span>).</p>
