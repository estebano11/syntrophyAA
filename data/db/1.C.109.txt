<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>1.C.109 The Bacterial Hemolysin A (TlyA) Family</strong></p>
<p>The hemolysins (TlyA proteins) of <em>Serpulina (Brachyspira) hyodysenteriae</em> are active at 27 to 40 
degrees C and pH 3 to 9 and are unaffected by enzymatic inhibitors. Pore
 formation was demonstrated by the inhibition of hemolysis with 
molecules of 2.0 to 2.3 nm in diameter and the release of <sup>86</sup>Rb &nbsp;from erythrocytes without hemoglobin release after exposure to native <span class='highlight'>hemolysin (<span id='ref10133.9353080' class='cite'>Hyatt and Joens 1997</span>)</span>. TlyA sequences and their conserved domains in mycobacterial proteins revealed the basis for their pore-forming activities and further confirmed their roles as virulence proteins (<span id='ref34554326.35021968' class='cite'>Sharma and Singh 2022</span>).</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Arenas, N.E., L.M.
