<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>1.C.53 The Lactocyclicin Q (Lactocyclicin Q) Family</strong></p>
<p style='text-align: left;'><em>Lactococcus</em> sp. strain QU 12,  isolated from cheese, produces
a cyclic bacteriocin termed lactocyclicin Q (<span id='ref6451.19139222' class='cite'>Sawa <em>et al.</em>, 2009</span>). Lactocyclicin
Q was purified from culture supernatant and has a unique antimicrobial spectrum, high level of
protease resistance, and heat stability compared to other reported
bacteriocins of lactic acid bacteria. This compound is
composed of 61 aas that have a cyclic structure with
linkage between the N and C termini by a peptide bond. It shows little similarity to any other antimicrobial peptides, including cyclic
bacteriocins, but shows significant similarity to small proteins from <em>Bacillus coagulans </em>(EEN93214; 38% identity) and <em>Paenibacillus larvae </em>(ZP_02326427; 36% identity). It has 1 or 2 TMSs.</p>
<p style='text-align: left;'>The second subfamily (1.C.53.2) consists of bacterial proteins, all of which probably have 2 TMSs, fairly centrally located, but variable is size.&nbsp; They derive from a variety of bacteria and are distantly related to the proteins in subfamily 1. The annotations for these proteins vary considerable from DUF1269, general stress protein, ChaB family protein, and permease.</p>
