<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='CENTER'><strong>1.E.19 The <em>Clostridium difficile</em> TcdE Holin (TcdE Holin) Family</strong></p>
<p>Toxigenic strains of <em>C. difficile</em> produce two large toxins (TcdA and TcdB) encoded within a pathogenicity locus.  <em>tcdE,</em> encoded between <em>tcdA</em> and <em>tcdB,</em> encodes a 166 aa protein which causes death to <em>E. coli</em> when expressed, and the structure of TcdE resembles holins.  TcdE acts on the bacterial membrane.  Since TcdA and TcdB lack signal peptides, they may be released via TcdE either prior to or subsequent to cell lysis.  Other Clostridial species bear homologues of TcdE.&nbsp; This family belongs to the Phage holin 4 superfamily.&nbsp;</p>
<p>The transport reaction believed to be catalyzed by TcdE is:</p>
