<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>1.H.3.&nbsp; The 4 TMS Amastin (Amastin) Family</strong></p>
<p><span class='highlight'>Amastin</span> is a 
transmembrane glycoprotein found on the cell surfaces of trypanosomatid 
parasites. Encoded by a large, diverse gene family, <span class='highlight'>amastin</span> was initially described from the intracellular, amastigote stage of 
<em>Trypanosoma cruzi</em> and <em>Leishmania donovani</em>. Genome sequences
subsequently showed that the <span class='highlight'>amastin</span> repertoire is much larger in <em>Leishmania</em> relative to <em>Trypanosoma</em>.&nbsp;<span id='ref34550908.19748930' class='cite'>Jackson 2010</span> sequenced the genomic 
regions containing <span class='highlight'>amastin</span> loci from two related insect parasites (<em>Leptomonas seymouri</em> and <em>Crithidia</em> sp.) and estimated the phylogeny for these and other <span class='highlight'>amastin</span> sequences. A<span class='highlight'>mastin</span> includes four subfamilies with distinct genomic positions, secondary 
structures, and evolution, which were already differentiated in the 
ancestral trypanosomatid. Diversification in <em>Leishmania</em> was initiated 
from a single ancestral locus on chromosome 34, with rapid derivation of
 novel loci through transposition and accelerated sequence divergence. 
This is absent from related organisms showing that diversification 
occurred after the origin of <em>Leishmania</em>. Some <span class='highlight'>amastin</span> genes may have evolved novel functions crucial to cell function in leishmanial parasites after the acquisition of a vertebrate host (<span id='ref34550908.19748930' class='cite'>Jackson 2010</span>). Amastins may be virulence genes in <em>Leishmania</em> species (<span id='ref34550909.19416551' class='cite'>Azizi <em>et al.</em> 2009</span>). Amastins have been used for vaccine development (<span id='ref34550910.21276444' class='cite'>Bolhassani <em>et al.</em> 2011</span>).</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Azizi, H., K.
