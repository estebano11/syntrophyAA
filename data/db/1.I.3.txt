<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>1.I.3.&nbsp; The Bacterial (Planctomycetes) Nuclear Pore-like Complex (B-NPC) Family&nbsp; </strong></p>
<p>Planctomycetes are distinguished from other Bacteria by 
compartmentalization of cells via internal membranes.&nbsp; The planctomycete <em>Gemmata obscuriglobus </em>contains a <span class="highlight">nuclear</span> body-like compartment, and thus possesses a type of cell organization with parallels to the eukaryote nucleus.&nbsp;<span id="ref34546908.28146565" class="cite">Sagulenko <em>et al.</em> 2017</span> showed that <span class="highlight">pore</span>-like structures occur in internal membranes of<em> G.obscuriglobus</em> and that they have elements structurally similar to those of eukaryote <span class="highlight">nuclear</span> pores, including a basket, ring-spoke structure, and eight-fold 
rotational symmetry. Bioinformatic analysis of proteomic data revealed
that some of the <em>G. obscuriglobus</em> proteins associated with <span class="highlight">pore</span>-containing membranes possess structural domains found in eukaryote <span class="highlight">nuclear pore</span> complexes. Moreover, immunogold labelling demonstrates localization of <span class="highlight">one</span> such protein, containing a &beta;-propeller domain, specifically to the <em>G. obscuriglobus </em><span class="highlight">pore</span>-like structures. Finding bacterial pores within internal cell membranes and with structural similarities to eukaryote <span class="highlight">nuclear pore</span> complexes raises the dual possibilities of either hitherto undetected homology or stunning evolutionary convergence (<span id="ref34546908.28146565" class="cite">Sagulenko <em>et al.</em> 2017</span>).</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Sagulenko, E., A.
