<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>1.S.2.&nbsp; The Bacterial Microcompartment Shell/Pore-forming Protein-2 (BMC-SP2) Family&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p>In contrast to the BMC-SP1 family which consists of small proteins of about 100 aas, the BMC-SP2 family consists of proteins of somewhat greater than 200 aas and are non-homologous to the BMC-SP1 proteins. However, they contribute to shell formation, sometimes in the same BMC shell as a BMC-SP1 protein as in the ethanolamine utilizing (Eut) BMC of <em>E. coli</em>, and both types of shell proteins form pores (<span id="ref34549074.20851901" class="cite">Takenoya <em>et al.</em> 2010</span>).&nbsp; In contrast to EutL (BMC-SP1), the pore of EutM (BMC-SP2) appears to be positively carged, indicating specificity for different solutes.&nbsp; Moreover, only the pore of EutL can be triggered to open by exposure to zinc ions (<span id="ref34549075.20851901" class="cite">Takenoya <em>et al.</em> 2010</span>).</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Takenoya, M., K.
