<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>1.W.7.&nbsp; The (<em>Bacillus</em> Phage SPP1) Portal Protein 7 (PPP7) Family</strong>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The <em>Bacillus</em> phage SSP1 protein f<span>orms the portal vertex of the capsid (<span id='ref34550783.17363899' class='cite'>Lebedev <em>et al.</em> 2007</span>).
 This portal plays critical roles in head assembly, genome packaging, 
neck/tail attachment, and genome ejection. The portal protein 
multimerizes as a single ring-shaped homododecamer arranged around a 
central channel. and binds to the terminase subunits to form the packaging 
machine. It is necessary to ensure correct procapsid size during capsid 
assembly. Once the capsid is packaged with the DNA, the terminase 
complex is substituted by the connector proteins gp15. </span><span>DNA molecules of <em>B. subtilis</em> <span class='highlight'>phage</span> SPP1 exhibit terminal redundancy and are partially circularly permuted (<span id='ref34550796.109737' class='cite'>Morelli <em>et al.</em> 1979</span>). </span></p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><HR>This family belongs to the <A HREF="superfamily.php?id=106">Phage Portal Protein (PPP) Superfamily</A>.<BR><HR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Lebedev, A.A., M.H.
