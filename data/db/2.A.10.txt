<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p align='JUSTIFY'><span style='font-family: Times;'>The KdgT permease of <em>Erwinia chrysanthemi</em> is the only well-characterized member of the KdgT family although homologues are present in many bacteria. &nbsp;Thus, members of the family are present both in Gram-negative and Gram-positive bacteria (<span id='ref760.2684787' class='cite'>Allen <em>et al.</em> 1989</span>). The protein from <em>E. chrysanthemi</em> possesses 398 amino acids and probably traverses the membrane as </span>&alpha;<span style='font-family: Times;'>-helicies 10-12 times. Uptake is energized by proton symport (<span id='ref34544582.3571157' class='cite'>Condemine and Robert-Baudouy 1987</span>). </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
