<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>2.A.101 The Malonate Uptake (MatC) Family (Formerly UIT1)</strong></p>
<p style='text-align: left;'><span style='font-family: Times;'>The UIT1 family is a constituent of the IT 
superfamily (<span id='ref7514.12662917' class='cite'>Lolkema and Slotboom, 2003</span>; <span id='ref7515.14643936' class='cite'>Prakash <em>et al.</em>, 2003</span>; <span id='ref7516.10627041' class='cite'>Rabus <em>et al.</em>, 1999</span>).  It is the st308/DctB family of Lolkema and 
Slotboom (<span id='ref7514.12662917' class='cite'>2003</span>).
  It consists of proteins from Gram-negative and Gram-positive bacteria 
(e.g., <em>Xanthomonas, Rhizobium</em> and <em>Streptomyces</em> species).  
The proteins are of about 450 aas with 12-14 putative TMSs.  Closest 
functionally-characterized homologues are in the DASS (TC #2.A.47) 
family. One member of this family is a putative malonate transporter (<span id='ref7517.9826185' class='cite'>An and Kim, 1998</span>).</span></p>
