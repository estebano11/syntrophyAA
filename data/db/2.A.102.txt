<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>2.A.102 The 4-Toluene Sulfonate Uptake Permease (TSUP) Family&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p>The TSUP family is also referred to as the TauE/SafE/YfcA/DUF81/COG0730 Family.&nbsp; Although its members have not been rigorously characterized, evidence is available that at least some members function in the transport of sulfur containing organic compounds.&nbsp; These include sulfolactate which may be exported by the TauE protein of <em>Cupriavidus necator </em>(<span id='ref11248.17768248' class='cite'>Weinitschke et al. 2007</span>; <span id='ref11247.22797525' class='cite'>Mayer et al. 2012</span>)<em> </em>and sulfoacetate which may be exported by the SafE1 protein of<em> </em><em>Neptuniibacter caesariensis </em>(<span id='ref6339.18506422' class='cite'>Krejc&iacute;k et al. 2008</span>).<em>&nbsp; </em>Another member of the TSUP family, TsaS of <em>Comamonas testosteroni, </em>has been reported to function in the uptake of 4-toluene sulfonate (<span id='ref34544960.15176949' class='cite'>Mampel et al. 2004</span>).<em>&nbsp; </em>None of these functional assignments can be considered to be certain.<em> </em></p>
<p>The TSUP family has been characterized using bioinformatic approaches.&nbsp;&nbsp;<span id='ref11249.22192777' class='cite'>Shlykov <em>et al.</em> 2012</span>;  showed that prokaryotic members of the
 family outnumber the eukaryotic members substantially, and in 
prokaryotes, but not eukaryotes, extensive lateral gene transfer 
occurred during family evolution. Despite unequal representation, 
homologues from the three taxonomic domains of life share well-conserved
 motifs. A prototypical eight TMS topology apparently arose by 
intragenic duplication of a four transmembrane segment (TMS) unit. 
Possibly, a two TMS &alpha;-helical hairpin structure was the precursor of the
 4 TMS repeat unit. Genome context analyses confirmed the proposal of a 
sulfur-based compound transport role for many <span class='highlight'>TSUP</span> homologues, but functional outliers appear to be prevalent as well (<span id='ref11249.22192777' class='cite'>Shlykov <em>et al.</em> 2012</span>).&nbsp; The TSUP is an established member of the Transporter/Opsin/G protein-coupled receptor (TOG) superfamily (<span id='ref10490.23981446' class='cite'>Yee <em>et al.</em> 2013</span>).</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><HR>This family belongs to the <A HREF="superfamily.php?id=58">Transporter-Opsin-G protein-coupled receptor (TOG) Superfamily</A>.<BR><HR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Deutschbauer, A., M.N.
