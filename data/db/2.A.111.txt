<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'>&nbsp; <strong>2.A.111 The Na<sup>+/</sup>H<sup>+</sup> Antiporter-E (NhaE) Family<br /></strong></p>
<p>The NhaH family is a constituent of the IT superfamily (<span id='ref9893.12662917' class='cite'>Lolkema and Slotboom 2003</span>; <span id='ref9894.14643936' class='cite'>Prakash <em>et al.</em> 2003</span>; <span id='ref9895.10627041' class='cite'>Rabus <em>et al.</em> 1999</span>). It is the st321/AitJ family of Lolkema and Slotboom (<span id='ref9893.12662917' class='cite'>2003</span>). It consists of proteins from Gram-negative bacteria (e.g., <em>Leptospira</em>, <em>Azotobacter</em>, <em>Neisseria</em>, <em>Ralstonia</em>, <em>Chlorobium</em> and <em>Rhizobial</em> species). The proteins are of about 480 aas with 12-14 putative TMSs.</p>
<p>An open reading frame from the genome of <em>Neisseria meningitidis</em> displaying similarity with the NhaE type of Na<sup>+</sup>/H<sup>+</sup> antiporters was expressed in <em>E. coli</em> and characterized for sodium transport ability. The <em>N. meningitidis</em> antiporter (NmNhaE) was able to complement an <em>E. coli</em> strain devoid of Na<sup>+</sup>/H<sup>+</sup> antiporters (KNabc) with respect to the ability to grow in the presence of high concentrations of NaCl or LiCl. Ion transport assays in everted vesicles prepared from the KNabc strain expressing NmNhaE from a plasmid confirmed its ability to translocate Na<sup>+</sup> and Li<sup>+ </sup>(<span id='ref9896.23208205' class='cite'>Sousa <em>et al.</em> 2013</span>).</p>
<p>The generalized reaction catalyzed by NhaE is,</p>
