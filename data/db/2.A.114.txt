<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>2.A.114 The Peptide Transporter Carbon Starvation CstA (CstA) Family</strong></p>
<p style='text-align: left;'>The CstA family consists of proteins from bacteria, and archaea. These proteins are of various sizes and topologies. For example, CstA of <em>E. coli</em> has 701 aas with 18 putative TMSs.  It has a long N-terminal CstA domain and a short C-terminal DUF4161 domain.  This protein is encoded by a carbon starvation inducible gene <em>cstA</em> that is under cyclic AMP-CRP control. Circumstantial evidence suggested that it may be a peptide transporter (<a class='reflink' href='#ref10321'>Schultz and Matin, 1991</a>).  A <em>Campylobacter jejuni</em> homologue has been shown to transport di- and tripeptides (see TC# 2.A.114.1.5) (<a class='reflink' href='#ref10322'>Rasmussen <em>et al.</em>, 2013</a>).</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><HR>This family belongs to the <A HREF="superfamily.php?id=5">APC Superfamily</A>.<BR><HR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Behr, S., L.
