<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>2.A.118&nbsp;The Basic Amino Acid Antiporter (ArcD) Family</strong></p>
<p>The ArcD family is a constituent of the IT superfamily (<a class='reflink' href='#ref10536'>Lolkema and Slotboom, 2003</a>; <a class='reflink' href='#ref10537'>Prakash <em>et al.</em>, 2003</a>; <a class='reflink' href='#ref10538'>Rabus <em>et al.</em>, 1999</a>). It is the st313/AitC family of Lolkema and Slotboom (<a class='reflink' href='#ref10536'>2003</a>). It consists of proteins from Gram-negative and Gram-positive bacteria (e.g., <em>Streptococcus</em>, <em>Escherichia</em>, <em>Salmonella</em>, <em>Fusobacterium</em> and<em> Borrelia </em>species). The proteins are of about 480 aas with 10-12 putative TMSs. Functionally characterized homologues are in the DcuC (TC #2.A.61) and ArsB (TC #2.A.4) families. Some members of the family probably catalyze arginine/ornithine or citruline/ornithine antiport (<a class='reflink' href='#ref10539'>Gupta <em>et al.</em>, 2013</a>; <a class='reflink' href='#ref10540'>Rimaux <em>et al.</em>, 2013</a>).</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><HR>This family belongs to the <A HREF="superfamily.php?id=1">IT Superfamily</A>.<BR><HR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Gupta, R., J.
