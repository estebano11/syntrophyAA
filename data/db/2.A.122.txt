<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>2.A.122 The LrgB/CidB Holin-like Glycolate/Glycerate Transporter (LrgB/CidB/GGT) Family</strong></p>
<p>CidA and LrgA are a putative holin/antiholin pair in <em>Staphylococcus aureus</em>. LrgB is LrgA-associated (<em>lrgA</em> and <em>lrgB</em> are translationally coupled) while CidB is CidA-associated (<em>cidA</em> and <em>cidB</em> are similarly translationally coupled). Both have been reported to play a role in cell wall hydrolysis, possibly by regulating murein hydrolase export. &nbsp;The <em>cidABC</em> operon is controlled by CidR, encoded adjacent to the <em>cidABC</em> operon, and the <em>lrgABC</em> operon is controlled by LrgR, encoded upstream of the <em>lrgABC</em> operon. &nbsp;Cell lysis results in response to acetic acid accumulation in the medium (<span id='ref11250.16109930' class='cite'>Yang <em>et al.</em> 2005)</span>.&nbsp;</p>
<p>A longer homologue of the bacterial LrgB has been shown to be the plastidic glycolate glycerate transporter, PLGG1, of 512 aas and 12 TMSs in <em>Arabidopsis thaliana</em> (<span id='ref11251.23382251' class='cite'>Pick <em>et al.</em> 2013</span>). &nbsp;The last 5 TMSs are homologous to the 5 TMSs in several CidB and LrgB proteins of bacteria (see proteins 2.A.122.1.1 and 1.2). This plant protein may represent a fusion of the bacterial LrgA and LrgB proteins (<span id='ref11252.23083702' class='cite'>Wang and Bayles 2013</span>). &nbsp;Because of this function, it appears that at least this plant protein of 12 TMSs must be a secondary carrier.</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Ahn, S.J., K.C.
