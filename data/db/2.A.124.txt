<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>2.A.124 The Lysine Exporter (LysO) Family</strong></p>
<p>In <em>E. coli, argO</em> encodes an
 exporter for l-arginine (Arg) and its toxic analogue canavanine (CAN).&nbsp; It's transcriptional activation and repression occurs in response to Arg and l-lysine 
(Lys), respectively, mediated by the regulator ArgP. Accordingly, 
<em>argO</em> and <em>argP</em> mutants are CAN supersensitive (CAN(ss)).&nbsp; <em><span class='highlight'>ybjE</span></em> encodes a Lys 
exporter as reported by Ueda and coworkers (T. Ueda, Y. Nakai, 
Y. Gunji, R. Takikawa, and Y. Joe, U.S. patents 7,629,142 B2 [December 
2009] and 8,383,363 B1 [February 2013] and European patent 1,664,318 B1 
[September 2009]). <em><span class='highlight'>ybjE</span></em> was isolated as a multicopy suppressor of the CAN(ss) phenotype of a strain lacking ArgO (<span id='ref34544034.25845847' class='cite'>Pathania and Sardesai 2015</span>). The absence of <span class='highlight'>YbjE</span> did not confer a CAN(ss) phenotype but instead conferred 
hypersensitivity to the lysine antimetabolite, thialysine, and led to 
growth inhibition by the dipeptide lysylalanine, due to elevated cellular Lys content. <span class='highlight'>YbjE</span> overproduction resulted in Lys excretion and syntrophic cross-feeding 
of a Lys auxotroph. Constitutive overexpression of <em>argO</em> promoted Lys 
cross-feeding that is indicative of a latent Lys export potential of 
ArgO. Arg modestly repressed <em><span class='highlight'>ybjE</span></em> transcription in an ArgR-dependent manner, and ArgR displayed Arg-sensitive binding to the <em><span class='highlight'>ybjE</span></em> promoter region <em>in vitro</em>. The reciprocal repression of <em>argO</em> and <em><span class='highlight'>ybjE</span>,</em> respectively, by Lys and Arg confers the specificity for basic amino 
acid export by distinct paths.&nbsp; Such cross-repression contributes
 to maintenance of cytoplasmic Arg/Lys balance (<span id='ref34544034.25845847' class='cite'>Pathania and Sardesai 2015</span>).</p>
<p>The generalized reaction catalyzed by LysO) is:</p>
