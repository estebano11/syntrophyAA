<p>The arginine-aspartate-aspartate (RDD) family, representing a category of 
transmembrane proteins containing one highly conserved arginine and two aspartates (RDD) are wide spread in Nature.&nbsp;<span id="ref34551935.29922240" class="cite">Shao <em>et al.</em> 2018</span> characterized a member of this family from the moderate halophile <em>Halobacillus andaensis</em> NEAU-ST10-40<sup>T</sup> and reported that it functions as a novel Na<sup>+</sup>(Li<sup>+</sup>, K<sup>+</sup>)/H<sup>+</sup> antiporter. The highly conserved 
arginine and aspartate residues are important for the protein's cation-transporting activity.&nbsp; In fact, D154, R124, R129, and D158 are 
indispensable for Na<sup>+</sup>(Li<sup>+</sup>, K<sup>+</sup>)/H<sup>+</sup> antiport activity whereas neither R35 nor D42 is required for activity (<span id="ref34551935.29922240" class="cite">Shao <em>et al.</em> 2018</span>).</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Shao, L., H.
