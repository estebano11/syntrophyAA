<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>2.A.135.&nbsp; The Inorganic Cation (</strong><strong>Na<sup>+</sup>(Li<sup>+</sup>, K<sup>+</sup>)/H<sup>+</sup>) Antiporter (ICA) Family&nbsp; </strong></p>
<p>Genomic DNA was screened for novel Na<sup>+</sup>/H<sup>+</sup> antiporter genes from <em>Halomonas zhaodongensis</em> by selection in <em>Escherichia coli</em> KNabc lacking three major Na<sup>+</sup>/H<sup>+</sup> antiporters (<span id='ref34554810.28652569' class='cite'>Meng <em>et al.</em> 2017</span>). Co-expression of two genes designated <em>umpAB</em>, encoding 
paired homologous unknown membrane proteins belonging to DUF1538 family (Pfam # PF07556), were found to confer <em>E. coli </em> KNabc tolerance to 0.4 M NaCl and 30 mM LiCl, and an alkaline pH 
resistance at 8.0. UmpAB localize as a hetero-dimer in the cytoplasmic membrane and exhibits pH-dependent Na<sup>+</sup>(Li<sup>+</sup>, K<sup>+</sup>)/H<sup>+</sup> antiport activity at a wide pH range of 6.5 to 9.5 with an optimal pH 
at 9.0. Neither UmpA nor UmpB showed homology with previously known single-gene or 
multi-gene Na<sup>+</sup>/H<sup>+</sup> antiporters, and thus, UmpAB represents a novel two-component Na<sup>+</sup>(Li<sup>+</sup>, K<sup>+</sup>)/H<sup>+</sup> antiporter (<span id='ref34554810.28652569' class='cite'>Meng <em>et al.</em> 2017</span>).</p>
<p>Many other proteins proved to be members of this family. Those in subfamily 1 have 7 or (duplicated) 14 TMSs, while those in subfamily 2 have fewer TMSs, usually 4 or 5 TMSs.&nbsp; They may be of smaller sizes (as suggested by the large number of homologues of about this size) or may be fragments of the larger proteins.&nbsp; This family was previously assigned to TC# 9.A.336.</p>
