<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><FONT FACE='Times'><P ALIGN='CENTER'><B>2.A.14  The Lactate Permease (LctP) Family</b></p> </b> <P>  Proteins of the LctP family have been found in Gram-negative and Gram-positive bacteria as well as archaea.   One member of the family, from <I>E. coli,</i> is the lactate:H<SIZE=2 sup>+</sup></font> symporter (<span id="ref777.8407843" class="cite">Dong <em>et al</em>., 1993</span>).  Two closely related paralogues have been found encoded within the <I>E. coli</i> genome, and one of these has been shown to be the glycolate uptake permease (<span id="ref780.11283302" class="cite">Nunez <em>et al</em>., 2001</span>).  Both permeases transport the same acids (L- and D-lactate as well as glycolate), but their physiological substrates differ.  The lactate permease transports both L- and D-lactate while the glycolate permease transports glycolate under physiological conditions.  Regulatory effects determine the substrates transported <I>in vivo.</i>  </p> <P>  Transport reactions catalyzed by functionally characterized members of the LctP family include: </p> <P ALIGN='CENTER'> D- or L-lactate or glycolate (out) + H<SIZE=2><SUP>+</sup></font> (out) <size=2> &rarr; </font> D- or L-lactate or glycolate (in) + H<SIZE=2><SUP>+</sup></font> (in).</p>  <P><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><HR>This family belongs to the <A HREF="superfamily.php?id=1">IT Superfamily</A>.<BR><HR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Dong, J.M., J.S.
