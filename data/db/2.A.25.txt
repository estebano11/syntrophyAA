<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><FONT FACE='Times'><P ALIGN='CENTER'><b>2.A.25  The Alanine or Glycine:Cation Symporter (AGCS) Family</b></p> </b> </p> <P>  Members of the AGCS family transport alanine and/or glycine in symport with Na<sup><SIZE=2>+</sup></FONT> and or H<sup><SIZE=2>+</sup></FONT>. The proteins are of 445-542 amino acyl residues in length and possess 8-12 putative transmembrane &alpha;-helical spanners.  They may possess 11 TMSs as seems to be true for DagA and AgcS, although Acp has only 8 (truncated?).  They are found in bacteria and archaea. Only three members of the family have been functionally characterized. These proteins show limited sequence similarity in the APC family (TC# 2.A.3), and homology has been established.  </p> <P>  The generalized transport reaction catalyzed by the AGCS family is: </p> <P ALIGN='CENTER'> alanine or glycine (out) + Na<sup><SIZE=2>+ </sup></FONT> or H<sup><SIZE=2>+</sup></FONT> (out)  &rarr;  alanine or glycine (in) + Na<sup><SIZE=2>+</sup></FONT> or H<sup><SIZE=2>+</sup></FONT> (in).  </p>  <P><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><HR>This family belongs to the <A HREF="superfamily.php?id=5">APC Superfamily</A>.<BR><HR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Bualuang A., Kageyama H., Tanaka Y., Incharoensakdi A. and Takabe T.
