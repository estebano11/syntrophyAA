<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='CENTER'><span style='font-family: Times;'><strong>2.A.26 The Branched Chain Amino Acid:Cation Symporter (LIVCS) Family</strong></span></p>
<p align='JUSTIFY'><span style='font-family: Times;'>Characterized members of this family transport all three of the branched chain aliphatic amino acids (leucine (L), isoleucine (I) and valine (V)). They are found in Gram-negative and Gram-positive bacteria and function by a Na</span><sup><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: x-small;'>+</span></sup><span style='font-family: Times;'> or H</span><sup><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: x-small;'>+</span></sup><span style='font-family: Times;'> symport mechanism. They possess about 440 amino acyl residues and display 12 putative transmembrane helical spanners. Some of these transporters can also take up threonine (<span id='ref34556392.37025642' class='cite'>Khozov <em>et al.</em> 2023</span>).&nbsp; BrnQ of <em>E. coli</em> has been reported to be able to take up threonine but not serine</span><span style='font-family: Times;'>.</span></p>
<p align='JUSTIFY'>The generalized transport reaction is:</p>
<p align='CENTER'><span style='font-family: Times;'>[L, I, V or T] (out) + [Na</span><sup><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: x-small;'>+</span></sup><span style='font-family: Times;'> or H</span><sup><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: x-small;'>+</span></sup><span style='font-family: Times;'>] (out) </span>&rarr;<span style='font-family: Times;'>&nbsp;[L, I, V or T] (in) + [Na</span><sup><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: x-small;'>+</span></sup><span style='font-family: Times;'> or H</span><sup><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: x-small;'>+</span></sup><span style='font-family: Times;'>] (in)</span></p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><HR>This family belongs to the <A HREF="superfamily.php?id=5">APC Superfamily</A>.<BR><HR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Braun, P.R., H.
