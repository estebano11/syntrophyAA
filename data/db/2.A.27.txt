<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='center'><span style='font-family: Times;'><strong>2.A.27 The Glutamate:Na<sup>+</sup><sup>&nbsp;</sup>Symporter (ESS) Family</strong></span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> Two members of this family have been functionally characterized.  Both permeases (called GltS) have 401-402 amino acyl residues with 12 putative &alpha;-helical transmembrane spanners.  Evidence for a 10 TMS topology has been obtained using translational <em>lacZ</em> and <em>phoA</em> fusions.  Both hydrophobic segments that did not prove to be TMSs were glycine-rich  (4 or 5 glycines per segment) (<span id='ref1948.' class='cite'>Gal <em>et al</em>., 2002</span>). The two domains, of 5 TMSs plus a reentrant or pore-loop each, suggest that these proteins along with CitS in the 2HCT family, (2.A.24) might be members of the 2HCT superfamily (<span id='ref5439.17269795' class='cite'>Dobrowolski <em>et al</em>., 2007</span>). The occurrence of these 'helix breaking' residues may account for their exclusion from the membrane. </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'><span style='font-family: Times;'> GltS is a Na<sup>+</sup>-dependent secondary carrier that transports L- and D-glutamate as well as the toxic analogues &alpha;-methyl glutamate and homocysteate (<span id='ref1949.6384422' class='cite'>Essenberg, 1984</span>).  Homologues are found in many diverse Gram-negative proteobacteria, <em>Synechocystis</em> and <em>Staphylococcus aureus.</em> The family is thus widespread in bacteria but not in archaea or eukaryotes. </span></span></p>
<p><span id='ref6710.19594131' class='cite'>Dobrowolski and Lolkema (2009)</span>&nbsp;have pointed to structural and mechanistic similarities between the ESS (TC #2.A.27) and 2-HCT (TC #2.A.24)
transporters, as well as the two domain structure of the
transporters and the presence and functional importance of the
reentrant loops present in both domains. They propose that the conserved GGXG motifs
are at the vertex of the reentrant loops.</p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
