<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='CENTER'><span style='font-family: Times;'><strong>2.A.35  The NhaC Na<sup>+</sup></strong></span><strong>:H<sup>+</sup> Antiporter (NhaC) Family</strong></p>
<p>Two members of the NhaC family have been functionally characterized.  One is believed to be a Na<sup>+</sup>:H<sup>+</sup> antiporter (<span id='ref996.9190799' class='cite'>Ito <em>et al</em>., 1997</span>); the other is a malate &middot; H<sup>+</sup>:lactate &middot;  Na<sup>+</sup> antiporter (<span id='ref997.10903309' class='cite'>Wei <em>et al</em>., 2000</span>).  Several paralogues are found in <em>Vibrio cholerae,</em> and two paralogues each are found encoded in the completely sequenced genomes of <em>Haemophilus influenzae</em> and <em>Bacillus subtilis.</em> <em>E. coli</em> lacks such a homologue. <em>Pyrococcus</em> species also have at least one homologue each.  Thus, members of the NhaC family are found in both Gram-negative bacteria and Gram-positive bacteria as well as archaea.  NhaC of <em>B. firmus</em> is 462 amino acyl residues long and possesses 12 putative transmembrane &alpha;-helical segments.  MleN of <em>B. subtilis</em> (468 aas) also exhibits 12 putative TMSs.</p>
<p>The transport reaction catalyzed by NhaC is probably:</p>
<p align='CENTER'>Na+ (in) + nH+ (out) ⇌&nbsp;Na+ (out) + nH+ (in). (n &gt; 1)</p>
<p>That catalyzed by MleN is probably:</p>
<p align='CENTER'>Malate (out) + H<sup>+</sup> (out) + Lactate (in) + Na<sup>+</sup> (in) ⇌&nbsp;Malate (in) + H<sup>+</sup> (in) + Lactate (out) + Na<sup>+</sup> (out)</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><HR>This family belongs to the <A HREF="superfamily.php?id=1">IT Superfamily</A>.<BR><HR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Ito, M., A.A.
