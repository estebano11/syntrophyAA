<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='CENTER'><span style='font-family: Times;'><strong>2.A.58  The Phosphate:Na<sup>+</sup> Symporter (PNaS) Family</strong></span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p>The PNaS family includes several functionally characterized, sodium-dependent, inorganic phosphate (P<sub>i</sub>) transporter (NPT2 or NptA) proteins from mammals.  Other animals, including fish and the worm, <em>C. elegans,</em> possess functionally uncharacterized homologues.  One closely related bacterial protein, NptA of <em>Vibrio cholerae,</em> resembles the animal proteins (34% identity; 51% similarity to many mammalian Npt2 symporters), but it has close homologues in many bacterial phyla.There are two subfamilies; one includes eukaryotic and prokaryotic proteins, and the other, only bacterial homologues.&nbsp; The bacterial proteins are found in many bacteria including <em>E. coli</em> (543 aas; TC# 2.A.58.2.1; 9 putative TMSs) and <em>Bacillus subtilis</em> (310 aas; TC# 2.A.58.2.5; 8 putative TMSs).  The bacterial homologue, NptA of <em>V. cholerae</em> has 10 putative TMSs.  The well-characterized mammalian proteins are found in renal (IIa isoform) and intestinal (IIb isoform) brush border membranes and are about 640 amino acyl residues long with 8-12 putative TMSs.  The N- and C-termini are in the cytoplasm, and a large hydrophilic loop is localized between TMSs 3 and 4.  While IIa isoforms are pH-dependent, IIb isoforms are pH-independent (de la <span id='ref2047.10692425' class='cite'>Horra <em>et al</em>., 2000</span>). Members of this family have been reported to have the CNT2 fold (<span id='ref34555317.36164651' class='cite'>Ferrada and Superti-Furga 2022</span>).</p>
<p>Mammalian porters of the PNaS family may catalyze cotransport of 3 Na<sup>+</sup> with 1 inorganic phosphate.  In response to parathyroid hormone and dietary inorganic phosphate, the renal cotransporter is rapidly inserted into and retrieved from the renal brush border membrane in a fashion similar to that by which the glucose transporter (Glut4) (TC# 2.A.1.1) is regulated by insulin, and aquaporins 1 and 2 (TC# 1.A.8.1) are regulated by vasopressin (Levi et al., 1999).  The renal type IIa PNaS member is a functional monomer (<span id='ref1209.10859311' class='cite'>Kohler et al., 2001</span>), but it interacts with PDZ proteins which probably mediate apical sorting, parathyroid hormone-controlled endocytosis and/or lysosomal sorting of internalized transporter (<span id='ref1931.11099500' class='cite'>Gisler <em>et al</em>., 2001</span>).&nbsp;</p>
<p>A single organism may have multiple paralogues of the PNaS family.  All of these proteins exhibit an internal repeat that probably arose by a tandem intragenic duplication event. Their properties and mechanisms of action have been reviewed (<span id='ref34549030.30244375' class='cite'>Forster 2019</span>).</p>
