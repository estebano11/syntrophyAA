<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='CENTER'><span style='font-family: Times;'><strong>2.A.61  The C<sub>4</sub></strong></span><strong>-dicarboxylate Uptake C (DcuC) Family</strong></p>
<p>A single functionally characterized protein comprises the DcuC family.  This is an anaerobic C<sub>4</sub>-dicarboxylate transporter (DcuC) of <em>E. coli.</em> A paralogue is encoded on the <em>E. coli</em> genome (455 amino acyl residues; 10 putative TMSs; spP45428), and a homologue is probably present in <em>Haemophilus influenzae.</em> The latter is included in the SwissProt database as two distinct but adjacent ORFs, HI0586 (145 aas; 4 putative TMSs; spP44019) and HI0585 (279 aas; 6 putative TMSs; spP44018).  These two putative ORFs may be the N- and C-terminal parts of a single protein, respectively.</p>
<p>The DcuC protein is induced only under anaerobic conditions and is not repressed by glucose. It may therefore function as a succinate efflux system during anaerobic glucose fermentation. However, when overexpressed, it can replace either DcuA or DcuB in catalyzing fumarate-succinate exchange and fumarate uptake.</p>
<p>The reactions probably catalyzed by the <em>E. coli</em> DcuC protein are:</p>
<p align='CENTER'>C<sub>4</sub>-dicarboxylate (out) + nH+ (out)  &rarr;  C<sub>4</sub>-dicarboxylate (in) + nH+ (in)</p>
<p align='CENTER'>and</p>
<p align='CENTER'>C<sub>4</sub>-dicarboxylate<sub>1</sub> (out) + C<sub>4</sub>-dicarboxylate<sub>2</sub> (in) ⇌</p>
