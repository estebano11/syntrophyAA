<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='CENTER'><strong>2.A.73 The Short Chain Fatty Acid Uptake (AtoE) Family </strong></p>
<p>AtoE of <em>E. coli</em> has been examined topologically (Daley et al., 2005).  It may transport short chain fatty acids into the <em>E. coli</em> cell (<span id='ref3337.4942769' class='cite'>Salanitro and Wegener, 1971</span>) although experimental characterisation is lacking. &nbsp;  Members of the AtoE derive from proteobacteria of the &alpha;-, &beta;-, &gamma;-, &delta;- and &epsilon;-subgroups, <em>Bacteroidetes</em>, Fusobacteria, Acidobacteria, Firmicutes, Actinobacteria and Archaea.  They are of fairly uniform size (425-487 aas). Topological analyses of AtoE family members suggest 10 TMSs in a 5 + 5 arrangement.  Phylogenetic analyses indicate that AtoE family members are diverse in function with several non-orthologous sets.  Further, the tree suggests either that horizontal transfer or early gene duplication followed by selective loss of genetic material in some organisms has occurred (T.E. Verlhac and M.H. Saier, unpublished results).</p>
<p>The reaction presumed to be catalyzed by AtoE is:</p>
