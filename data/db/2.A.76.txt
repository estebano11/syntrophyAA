<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='CENTER'><span style='font-family: Times;'><strong>2.A.76  The Resistance to Homoserine/Threonine (RhtB) Family</strong></span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p>Hundreds of sequenced proteins, derived from Gram-negative and Gram-positive bacteria as well as archaea, comprise the RhtB family, but few of these proteins are functionally characterized (<span id='ref1314.10943564' class='cite'>Vrljic <em>et al</em>., 1999</span>).  <em>E. coli</em> possesses five paralogues, and a large region of one of them (YahN of <em>E. coli</em>) exhibits significant sequence similiarity to YggA of <em>E. coli,</em> an established member of the LysE family (TC #2.A.75).  The PSI-BLAST program groups the LysE family (TC# 2.A.75), the RhtB family and the CadD family (TC #2.A.77) together.  These proteins are all of about the same size and apparent topology, further suggesting a common evolutionary origin.</p>
<p>The first two members of the RhtB family to be characterized functionally were the RhtB and RhtC permeases of <em>E. coli</em> (<span id='ref1313.10322417' class='cite'>Aleshin <em>et al</em>., 1999</span>; <span id='ref1315.10386596' class='cite'>Zakataeva <em>et al</em>., 1999</span>).  YfiK of <em>E. coli</em> exports cysteine, O-acetylserine and azaserine (<span id='ref2332.12562784' class='cite'>Franke <em>et al</em>., 2003</span>).  The YeaS (LeuE) homologue exports leucine and several other neutral, hydrophobic amino acids (<span id='ref3447.16098526' class='cite'>Kutukova <em>et al</em>., 2005</span>).  Aleshin et al. (<span id='ref1313.10322417' class='cite'>1999</span>) present a partial alignment of recognized bacterial and archael members of the RhtB and LysE families, but not the CadD family.  Vrljic et al. (<span id='ref1314.10943564' class='cite'>1999</span>) present phylogenetic trees for the original three families of the LysE superfamily (LysE, RhtB and CadD).</p>
<p>The transport reaction presumably catalyzed by members of the RhtB family is:</p>
