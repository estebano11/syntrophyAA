<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='CENTER'><span style='font-family: Times;'><strong>2.A.78  The Branched Chain Amino Acid Exporter (LIV-E) Family </strong></span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'>The LIV-E family consists of pairs of integral membane proteins.  Functional data are available for AzlC and AzlD of <em>Bacillus subtilis</em> which together comprise an efflux pump for branched chain amino acids, leucine, isoleucine and valine.  Overexpression of AzlC and AzlD (encoded within a single operon, the <em>azlBCDEF</em> operon) gives rise to resistance to 4-azaleucine.  The <em>azlE</em> gene encodes a homologue of the BrnQ proteins of <em>Lactobacillus delbrueckii</em> and <em>Corynebacterium glutamicum</em> which are branched chain amino acid:H<sup>+</sup></span> symport (uptake) permeases.</p>
<p>The homologous BrnE-BrnF system has been functionally characterized from <em>Corynebacterium glutamicum</em> (<a class='reflink' href='#ref2140'>Kennerknecht <em>et al</em>., 2002</a>; <a class='reflink' href='#ref3087'>Tr&ouml;tschel <em>et al</em>., 2005</a>).  Both proteins were shown to be required for activity, and efflux of L-methionine, isoleucine, leucine and valine was demonstrated.  These two proteins exhibit 7 and 4 putative TMSs, respectively.  Limited evidence suggests that these proteins may have arisen from an unusual gene triplication event in which a 4 TMS-encoding element triplicated to give 8 and 4 TMS-encoding elements, and that the N-terminal TMS in the larger protein was lost to give a 7 TMS protein (<a class='reflink' href='#ref2140'>Kennerknecht <em>et al</em>., 2002</a>).  The best inducer of <em>brnFE</em> operon expression is cytoplasmic L-methionine, suggesting that methionine is the native substrate.</p>
<p>Homologues of AzlC and AzlD are found in a diverse group of Gram-negative and Gram-positive bacteria as well as archaea.  Several Gram-negative &alpha;-proteobacteria have 2 or 3 paralogues.  Phylogenetic clustering of paralogues suggests that they are very divergent in sequence, resulting from early gene duplication events.  It is therefore anticipated that they will not serve a unified function.</p>
<p>The generalized transport reaction presumed to be catalyzed by members of the LIV-E family is:</p>
