<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='CENTER'><span style='font-family: Times;'><strong>2.A.79  The Threonine/Serine Exporter (ThrE) Family</strong></span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'>The first member of the ThrE family to be characterized was the ThrE protein of <em>Corynebacterium glutamicum&nbsp;</em>(<span id='ref7699.12089010' class='cite'>Simic <em>et al.</em>, 2002</span>). This protein (489aas) exhibits 10 putative TMSs and catalyzes the pmf-dependent efflux of threonine and serine (<span id='ref7700.12111147' class='cite'>Kruse <em>et al.</em>, 2002</span>). It has close homologues in <em>Mycobacterium tuberculosis</em> (Rv 3737; 529aas; pirB70798) and <em>Strepomyces coelicolor</em> (578aas; pirT36336. However more distant homologues, some of which consist of 'spliced' two-component systems, are also found in <em>E. coli, Vibrio cholera, Xylella fastidiosa, Saccharomyces cerevisiae, Schizosaccharomyces pombe</em> and <em>Methanobacterium thermoautotrophicum</em>. Thus, members of the ThrE family are diverse in sequence and are ubiquitous, being found in bacteria, archaea and eukaryotes (<span id='ref7536.12879215' class='cite'>Eggeling and Sahm, 2003;</span>&nbsp;<span id='ref7701.11881894' class='cite'>Yen <em>et al.</em>, 2002</span>). Other characterized members of this family catalyze succinate (and probably other dicarboxylate) transport (export; see TC# 2.A.79.2.1).<br /></span></p>
<p style='text-align: left;'><span style='font-family: Times;'>The transport reaction catalyzed by ThrE of <em>E. coli</em> (TC# 2.A.79.1.1) is:</span></p>
<p align='CENTER'><span style='font-family: Times;'>threonine or serine (in) + H</span><sup><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: x-small;'>+</span></sup><span style='font-family: Times;'>&nbsp;(out) ⇌&nbsp;threonine or serine (out) + H</span><sup><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: x-small;'>+</span></sup><span style='font-family: Times;'>&nbsp;(in)</span></p>
<p style='text-align: left;'>That for YjjPB of <em>E. coli</em> (TC# 2.A.79.2.1) is:</p>
<p align='CENTER'>dicarboxylate (succinate, and probably malate and fumarate) (in) + H<sup>+</sup> (out) ⇌ dicarboxylate (out) + H<sup>+</sup> (in).</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Bendezú, F.O. and P.A. de Boer.
