<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><FONT FACE='Times'><P ALIGN='CENTER'><b> 2.A.8 The Gluconate:H<SIZE=2 sup>+</sup></font> Symporter (GntP) Family</b></p> </b> <P>  Protein members of the GntP family include known gluconate permeases of <I>E. coli</i> and <I>Bacillus</i> species (<span id="ref1319.9119199" class="cite">Peekhaus <em>et al</em>., 1997</span>; <span id="ref1320.1659648" class="cite">Reizer <em>et al</em>., 1991</span>).  Four of the seven <I>E. coli</i> paralogues have been found to possess active gluconate uptake activity, and one of them (GntW) can accommodate both L-idonate and D-gluconate although L-idonate is the physiological substrate (<span id="ref2024.9658018" class="cite">Bausch <em>et al</em>., 1998</span>).  Another (GntP) transports D-gluconate with high affinity but is specifically induced by and transports D-fructuronate (Bates <span id="ref2949.15516583" class="cite">Utz <em>et al</em>., 2004</span>).  GntT of <i>E. coli</i> is the physiological gluconate permease (<span id="ref2948.9045817" class="cite">Porco <em>et al</em>., 1997</span>).  These proteins are of about 450 residues and possess 12 or 14 putative transmembrane &alpha;-helical spanners.  The GntP family is in the IT superfamily (<span id="ref2947.14643936" class="cite">Prakash <em>et al</em>., 2003</span>).  </p> <P>  The generalized transport reaction catalyzed by proteins of the GntP family is:  </p> <P ALIGN='CENTER'> Carbohydrate acid (out) + nH<SIZE=2><sup>+</sup></font> (out)  &rarr;  Carbohydrate acid  (in) + nH<SIZE=2><sup>+</sup></font> (in). </p>  <P><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><HR>This family belongs to the <A HREF="superfamily.php?id=1">IT Superfamily</A>.<BR><HR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Anfora, A.T., and R.A.
