<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>&nbsp;</strong><span style='font-weight: bold; font-family: Times;'>2.A.86  The Autoinducer-2 Exporter (AI-2E) Family (formerly the PerM Family, TC #9.B.22)</span></p>
<p><strong>&nbsp;</strong><span style='font-family: Times;'> The AI-2E family (UPF0118) is a large family of prokaryotic proteins derived from a variety of bacteria and archaea.  Those examined are about 350 residues in length, and the couple that have been examined exhibit 7 putative TMSs (<span id='ref7380.20559013' class='cite'>Rettner and Saier, 2010</span>). <em>E. coli</em>, <em>B. subtilis</em> and several other prokaryotes have multiple paralogues encoded within their genomes. Herzberg et al. (<a class='reflink' href='#ref3247'>2006</a>) have presented strong evidence for a role of a AI-2E family homologue, YdgG (renamed TqsA) is an exporter of the <em>E. coli</em> autoinducer-2 (AI-2) (<a class='reflink' href='#ref3246'>Camilli and Bassler, 2006</a>; <a class='reflink' href='#ref3272'>Chen <em>et al</em>., 2002</a>).  AT-2 is a proposed signalling molecule for interspecies communication in bacteria.  It is a furanosyl borate diester (<a class='reflink' href='#ref3272'>Chen <em>et al</em>., 2002</a>). It is induced in<em> Bacillus subtilis</em> by exposure to rice seedlings (<span id='ref34545581.25651892' class='cite'>Xie <em>et al.</em> 2015</span>).&nbsp; </span><span style='font-family: Times;'>AI-2, a universal molecule for both intra- and 
inter-species communication, is involved in the regulation of biofilm 
formation, virulence, motility, chemotaxis, and antibiotic resistance. (</span><span style='font-family: Times;'><span style='font-weight: normal;'><span id='ref34554876.35698912' class='cite'>Khera <em>et al.</em> 2022</span></span>).<br />&nbsp;&nbsp; More recently, it has been reported that this family includes a member of the </span>UPF0118 family (which was the former designation for the AI-2E family), and this transmembrane protein with 7 TMSs, exhibits reversible pH-dependent Na<sup>+</sup> or Li<sup>+</sup>/H<sup>+</sup> antiport activity. Phylogenetic analyses were reported (<span id='ref34549610.28374790' class='cite'>Dong <em>et al.</em> 2017</span>).&nbsp; Thus, it appears that different members of the family may have very different transport functions.<span style='font-weight: normal;'> Cryo-EM structures of two pentameric autoinducer-2 exporter from <em>E. coli </em>(TqsA (TC# 2.A.86.1.4) and YdiK (TC# 2.A.86.2.1) revealed the probable transport mechanism (<span id='ref34554876.35698912' class='cite'>Khera <em>et al.</em> 2022</span>). Each of the 5 subunits is believed to be a functional unit, and an elevator-type mechanism has been suggested.</span></p>
