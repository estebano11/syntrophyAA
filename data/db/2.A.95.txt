<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='center'><span style='font-family: Times;'><strong> 2.A.95 The 6 TMS Neutral Amino Acid Transporter (NAAT) Family </strong></span></p>
<p>A gene encoding a small neutral amino acid transporter was cloned from the genome of the hyperthermophilic archaeon <em>Thermococcus</em> sp. KS-1.&nbsp; The cloned gene, <em>snatA</em>, encodes a protein of 216 amino acid residues, SnatA, with six membrane-spanning segments. <em>E. coli</em> AK430 cells transformed with <em>snatA</em> transported glycine with an apparent K(t) value of 24 &mu;M. Competition studies indicated that SnatA transports various L-type neutral amino acids.&nbsp; Glycine transport is inhibited by a protonophore, FCCP, or valinomycin plus nigericin, indicating that the process is dependent on an electrochemical potential of H<sup>+</sup>.(<span id='ref5374.12761179' class='cite'>Akahane <em>et al.</em> 2003</span>).</p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'>MarC is encoded by a gene at the multiple antibiotic resistance (<em>mar</em>) locus.  The <em>mar</em> locus consists of two divergently positioned transcriptional units that flank the operator, <em>marO,</em> in both <em>E. coli</em> and <em>Salmonella typhimurium.</em> One transcription unit encodes MarC, an integral inner membrane protein with 6 established TMSs with the N- and C-termini in the cytoplasm (<a class='reflink' href='#ref5372'>Drew <em>et al</em>., 2002</a>).  Its function is unknown. The other unit consists of an operon, <em>marRAB,</em> encoding (1) the MarR repressor which binds <em>marO</em> and negatively regulates <em>marRAB</em> expression, (2) MarA, a transcriptional activator that activates expression of other genes such as <em>acrAB</em> (encoding the principal <em>E. coli</em> multidrug efflux pump of the RND superfamily (TC #2.A.6.2)) and the <em>mar</em> regulon itself, and (3) MarB, a small protein of 71 amino acyl residues of unknown function.  A periplasmic binding protein, MppA, essential for the uptake of the cell wall murein tripeptide, </span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>L</span><span style='font-family: Times;'>-alanyl-</span>&gamma;<span style='font-family: Times;'>-</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>D</span><span style='font-family: Times;'>-glutamyl-<em>meso</em>-diaminopimelate via the Opp permease, regulates <em>mar</em> regulon expression. Loss of MppA causes overproduction of MarA which activates <em>acrAB</em>, causing pleiotropic drug resistance. MppA probably functions upstream of MarA in a signal transduction pathway that negatively controls expression of the <em>marRAB</em> operon.</span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> Homologues of MarC are found in numerous Gram-negative and Gram-positive bacteria including many human pathogens. Several archaea also encode MarC homologues. Some of these organisms have 2 or more paralogues. Most of these proteins are of about the same size (180-230 aas) although a few are larger. They exhibit 6 (or in some cases, possibly 5) putative TMSs. </span></p>
