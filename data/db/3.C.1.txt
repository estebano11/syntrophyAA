<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='CENTER'><span style='font-family: Times;'><strong>3.C.1 The Na<sup>+</sup><strong> Transporting Methyltetrahydromethanopterin:Coenzyme M Methyltransferase (NaT-MMM) Family</strong></strong></span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> The NaT-MMM (EC 2.1.1.86) of <em>Methanobacterium thermoautotrophicum</em>has been purified and characterized, and the Na<sup>+</sup></span> transport reaction has been studied in inverted membrane vesicles.  Sequencing of the encoding genes revealed eight contiguous genes organized in an operon.  The majority of the encoded subunits are probably integral membrane proteins.  Thus, MtrA, B, F and G have 1 TMS; MtrC has 7; MtrD has 6; MtrE has 6; and MtrH has none (<span id='ref1041.11248186' class='cite'>Gottschalk and Thauer, 2001</span>).  MtrE is believed to be the Na<sup>+</sup>-transporting subunit.  MtrA bears a cob(I)amide prosthetic group which is methylated and demethylated in the catalytic cycle.  Demethylation is the Na<sup>+</sup>-dependent step.   Thus, methyl transfer from methylated MtrA to coenzyme M drives the electrogenic translocation of Na<sup>+</sup>.  The overall methyl transfer reaction is strongly exergonic (&Delta;<span style='font-family: Times;'>G&deg;= -30 kJ/mol) (<span id='ref1041.11248186' class='cite'>Gottschalk and Thauer, 2001</span>). </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> The NaT-MMM complex probably contains iron/sulfur clusters.  As noted above, the enzyme catalyzes two chemical reactions as follows: </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p align='Center'><span style='font-family: Times;'> (1) CH<sub>3</sub>-H<sub>4</sub>MPT + E:Co(I) &rarr;&nbsp;H<sub>4</sub>MPT + E:CH<sub>3</sub>-Co(III).</span></p>
<p align='Center'><span style='font-family: Times;'> (2) E:CH<sub>3</sub>-Co(III) + HS-CoM </span> &rarr;&nbsp;<span style='font-family: Times;'>E:Co(I) + CH<sub>3</sub>-S-CoM. </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> Reaction 1 is reversible, but reaction 2 is essentially irreversible in the direction shown.  Na<sup>+</sup> efflux is probably coupled to the second of these two reactions. </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> The generalized transport reaction is therefore probably: </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p align='CENTER'><span style='font-family: Times;'>Na<sup>+</sup> (in) + E:CH<sub>3</sub>-Co(III) + HS-CoM </span> &rarr;<span style='font-family: Times;'>&nbsp;Na<sup>+</sup> (out) + E:Co(I) + CH<sub>3</sub>-S-CoM.</span></p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Becher, B., V.
