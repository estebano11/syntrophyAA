<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='CENTER'><strong>3.D.8  The Na<sup>+</sup> or H<sup>+</sup> Pumping Formyl Methanofuran Dehydrogenase (FMF-DH) Family</strong></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p align='JUSTIFY'><span style='font-family: Times;'> FMF-DH in <em>Methanosarcina barkeri</em> couples Na</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>+</span><span style='font-family: Times;'> transport to the reversible CO</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>2</span><span style='font-family: Times;'> reduction-dependent formylation of the archaeal cofactor, methanofuran (MF). This enzyme thus initiates methanogenesis during growth in H</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>2 </span><span style='font-family: Times;'>and CO</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>2</span><span style='font-family: Times;'>, but it is also required for methanol and methylamine utilization, in which case formyl methanofuran is oxidized to CO</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>2</span><span style='font-family: Times;'> and methanofuran. During CO</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>2</span><span style='font-family: Times;'> reduction to CH</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>4</span><span style='font-family: Times;'>, a *&micro; (Na</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>+</span><span style='font-family: Times;'>) is established when the Na</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>+</span><span style='font-family: Times;'>/H</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>+</span><span style='font-family: Times;'> antiporter is inhibited.  Formaldehyde reduction to CH</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>4</span><span style='font-family: Times;'> is coupled to the extrusion of 4Na</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>+</span><span style='font-family: Times;'>, catalyzed by the H</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>4</span><span style='font-family: Times;'>MPT:HS-CoM methyl transferase (TC# 10.1). However, about 2Na</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>+</span><span style='font-family: Times;'> are consumed during CO</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>2</span><span style='font-family: Times;'> reduction. Consequently a net ~2Na</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>+</span><span style='font-family: Times;'> are pumped per CH</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>4</span><span style='font-family: Times;'> reduced. By contrast, the <em>Methanosarcina mazei </em>G&ouml;l FMF-DH may couple formyl MF oxidation to H</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>+</span><span style='font-family: Times;'> rather than Na</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>+ </span><span style='font-family: Times;'> extrusion.</span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p align='JUSTIFY'><span style='font-family: Times;'> The <em>M. barkeri</em> FMF DH catalyzes extrusion of ~2Na</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>+ </span><span style='font-family: Times;'> per mole of formaldehyde oxidized to CO</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>2</span><span style='font-family: Times;'> + 2H</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>2</span><span style='font-family: Times;'>, and consequently, a membrane potential of ~100mV (inside negative) builds up. This potential can be used for ATP synthesis via a Na</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>+</span><span style='font-family: Times;'>/H</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>+</span><span style='font-family: Times;'> antiporter and a H</span><span style='font-family: Times; font-size: xx-small;'>+</span><span style='font-family: Times;'>-translocating ATP synthase. The functions of the individual subunits in the multisubunit formyl MF dehydrogenase are not well characterized, but several are homologous to the subunits of other dehydrogenases.</span></p>
