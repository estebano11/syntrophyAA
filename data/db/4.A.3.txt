<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='center'><strong>4.A.3  The PTS Lactose-N,N'-Diacetylchitobiose-&beta;-glucoside (Lac) Family </strong></p>
<p>The Lac family includes several sequenced lactose (&beta;-galactoside) porters of Gram-positive bacteria as well as the <em>E. coli</em> and <em>Borrelia burgdorferi</em> N,N'-diacetylchitobiose (Chb) porters.  The former can transport aromatic &beta;-glucosides and cellobiose as well as the chitin disaccharide, Chb.  However, only Chb induces expression of the <em>chb</em> operon.  While the Lac porters consist of two polypeptide chains (IIA and IICB), the Chb porters of <em>E. coli</em> and <em>B. burgdorferi</em> consist of three (IIA, IIB and IIC).  In <em>E. coli,</em> the IIA<sup>Chb</sup> protein has been shown to form a stable dimer both when phosphorylated and when unphosphorylated.&nbsp; The IIC domains of these permeases are believed to have a uniform topology with 10 TMSs (<span id='ref10592.21471968' class='cite'>Cao <em>et al.</em> 2011</span>; <span id='ref34545337.27276425' class='cite'>Vastermark and Saier 2016</span>).</p>
<p>In <em>E. coli</em>, the IIB<sup>Chb</sup> is a monomer.  Two IIB<sup>Chb</sup> monomers associate with the IIA<sup>Chb</sup> dimer.  The structure of the IIB domain of the Chb porter has been determined both by NMR and by x-ray crystallography.  It exhibits an &alpha;/&beta; doubly wound superfold.  This is different from the structure of the IIB<sup>Glc</sup> and IIB<sup>Man</sup> domains.  IIB<sup>Sgc</sup>, believed to function in pentose transport, is homologous to IIB<sup>Lac</sup> and IIB<sup>Chb</sup>.  In <em>B. subtilis,</em> a PTS porter similar to the Chb porter of <em>E. coli</em> is believed to transport lichenan (a &beta;-1,3;1,4 glucan) degradation products, oligosaccharides of 2-4 glucose units.  The <em>B. burgdorferi</em> system is more similar to the <em>Bacillus</em> Lic system than the <em>E. coli</em> Chb system. The IIC domains of members of the Lac family are all more similar to each other than they are to those of the Glc, Bgl, Fru and Mtl families.</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><HR>This family belongs to the <A HREF="superfamily.php?id=8">PTS-GFL Superfamily</A>.<BR><HR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Ab, E., G.
