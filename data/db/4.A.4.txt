<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='center'><strong> 4.A.4 The PTS Glucitol (Gut) Family </strong></p>
<p>The Gut family consists only of glucitol-specific porters, but these occur both in Gram-negative and Gram-positive bacteria. II<sup>Gut</sup> of <em>E. coli</em> consists of three proteins, a IIA protein, a putative 4 TMS IIC2 protein and a putative 4 TMS IIC1 protein (<span id='ref6398.17114898' class='cite'>Nguyen <em>et al.</em>, 2006</span>). The N- and C-termini as well as the IIB domain may thereby be localized to the cell cytoplasm, but the topology has not been established experimentally. IIA<sup>Gut</sup> is believed to be phosphorylated on a histidyl residue, while IIB<sup>Gut</sup> is probably phosphorylated on a cysteyl residue. However, these possibilities have not been demonstrated experimentally.</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><HR>This family belongs to the <A HREF="superfamily.php?id=8">PTS-GFL Superfamily</A>.<BR><HR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Nguyen, T.X., M.R.
