<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='center'><strong> 4.A.5 The PTS Galactitol (Gat) Family </strong></p>
<p>The only well-characterized member of the Gat famly is the galactitol permease of <em>E. coli </em>(<span id='ref1664.7772602' class='cite'>Noblemann and Lengeler, 1995</span>)<em>.</em> However, a homologous IIC protein (38% identity) from <em>Listeria monocytogenes</em> has been shown to be required for D-arabitol fermentation (<span id='ref1668.11316371' class='cite'>Saklani-Jusforgues <em>et al</em>., 2001</span>). It functions together with IIA<sup>Gat</sup> and IIB<sup>Gat</sup> homologues. IIC<sup>Gat</sup> is distantly related to IIC<sup>Sgc</sup> of <em>E. coli</em> (<span id='ref6790.8973315' class='cite'>Reizer <em>et al.</em>, 1996</span>); IIA<sup>Gat</sup> is distantly related to IIA<sup>Sga</sup> and IIA<sup>Sgc</sup> of <em>E. coli</em> as well as IIA<sup>Mtl</sup> and IIA<sup>Fru</sup>. IIB<sup>Gat</sup> is distantly related to IIB<sup>Sga</sup> and IIB<sup>Sgc</sup> of <em>E. coli.</em> Domains in the LicR/CelR family of transcriptional activators show C-terminal domains exhibiting weak sequence similiarity to IIB<sup>Gat</sup> and IIA<sup>Gat </sup> (<span id='ref1669.11361063' class='cite'>Tchieu <em>et al.</em>, 2001</span>). The biochemistry of this family is poorly defined. Howeever, the 3-d structure of GatB has been solved. GatB consists of a central four-stranded parallel beta-sheet flanked by alpha-helices on both sides. The active site cysteine of GatB is located at the beginning of an unstructured loop between beta1 and alpha1 that folds into a P-loop-like structure. This structural arrangement shows similarities with other IIB subunits as noted above, but also with mammalian low molecular weight protein tyrosine phosphatases (LMW PTPase) and arsenate reductase (ArsC) (<span id='ref34546900.16963640' class='cite'>Volpon <em>et al.</em> 2006</span>).The Gat Family is related to the L-Asc Family (TC# 4.A.7) (<span id='ref6778.15153772' class='cite'>Hvorup <em>et al.</em> 2003;</span> <span id='ref34546901.15667312' class='cite'>Saier <em>et al.</em> 2005</span>).</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><HR>This family belongs to the <A HREF="superfamily.php?id=4">PTS-AG Superfamily</A>.<BR><HR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Hvorup, R., A.B.
