<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='CENTER'><strong> 4.A.7 The PTS L-Ascorbate (L-Asc) Family</strong></p>
<p>A single PTS permease of the L-Asc family of PTS permeases has been functionally characterized.  This is the SgaTBA system (<span id='ref2288.11361063' class='cite'>Tschieu <em>et al</em>., 2002</span>), renamed UlaABC (<span style='text-decoration: underline;'>u</span>tilization of <span style='text-decoration: underline;'>L</span>-<span style='text-decoration: underline;'>a</span>scorbate) by Yew and Gerlt (<span id='ref2289.11741871' class='cite'>2002</span>).  The SgaTBA permease consists of three proteins: SgaT, SgaB, and SgaA.  SgaT is a 12 TMS protein, possibly very distantly related to the MFS hexuronate permease of <em>E. coli</em> (TC #2.A.1.14.2).  It presumably functions as a PTS IIC protein.  This gene product as well as SgaB (homologous to but distantly related to IIB proteins of the PTS LAC family (TC #4.A.3)) and SgaA (homologous to but distantly related to IIA proteins of the PTS FRU family (TC #4.A.2)), are all essential for anaerobic L-ascorbate utilization, transport and phosphorylation (<span id='ref2290.12644495' class='cite'>Zhang <em>et al</em>., 2003</span>).  This is the first documented example where the two sugar-specific energy-coupling proteins of a PTS permease are more closely related to the proteins of two different families.  The <em>sga</em> regulation is controlled by the nearby YjfQ repressor (<span id='ref2291.12374842' class='cite'>Campos <em>et al</em>., 2002</span>; <span id='ref2290.12644495' class='cite'>Zhang <em>et al</em>., 2003</span>).</p>
<p>Homologues of SgaT, like other PTS protein homologues, have been identified in a large number of evolutionarily divergent bacteria but not in eukaryotes (<span id='ref2290.12644495' class='cite'>Zhang <em>et al</em>., 2003</span>).  Bacteria which encode SgaT homologues include numerous Gram-negative proteobacteria as well as many low and high G+C Gram-positive bacteria.  Except for species of <em>Corynebacterium, Streptomyces</em> and <em>Bacillus,</em> almost all organisms possessing SgaTBA homologues are human/animal pathogens.  Several organisms have two or more SgaT paralogues including <em>E. coli</em> which has three.  In <em>E. coli,</em> the SgaTBA homologues cannot transport L-ascorbate since the the <em>sgaA, sgaB</em> and <em>sgaT</em> mutants proved to be negative for L-ascorbate utilization, uptake and phosphorylation.  In some of the homologues found in other bacteria, SgaB domains are fused C-terminal to the SgaT domains.  For example, this is true of putative transporters in <em>Vibrio cholerae</em> (AAP96157; 586 aas), <em>Pasteurella multocida</em> (AAK02848; 625 aas) and <em>Mycoplasma pulmonis</em> (CAC13371; 650 aas).  Homologues of SgaB and SgaA, but not SgaT, are also found in transcriptional activator proteins where they function in regulation rather than sugar transport (<span id='ref2285.12437213' class='cite'>Greenberg <em>et al</em>., 2002</span>).</p>
<p>The L-Asc Family is related to the Gat Family (TC# 4.A.5). The discovery of a nonhomologous, nontransporting Enzyme II complex specific for dihydroxyacetone resembling in sequence functionally characterized ATP-dependent dihydroxyacetone kinases (<span id='ref2286.11350937' class='cite'>Gutknecht <em>et al</em>., 2001</span>) illustrates the versatility of the PTS in recruiting proteins that evolved for other catalytic purposes into this PEP-dependent phosphotransferase system (<span id='ref2646.15153772' class='cite'>Hvorup <em>et al.</em> 2003</span>; <span id='ref34546901.15667312' class='cite'>Saier <em>et al.</em> 2005</span>).  Since not all established Enzyme II complexes are homologous (<span id='ref2287.7815935' class='cite'>Saier and Reizer, 1994</span>), the use of SgaT as an Enzyme IIC of the PTS, while representing a unique and novel example, does not establish a new principle.  Nevertheless, the mechanism of phosphoryl transfer from SgaB-P to the substrate sugar acid may well prove to exhibit unique features.</p>
