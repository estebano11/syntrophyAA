<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>4.F.1 The Choline/Ethanolaminephosphotransferase 1 (CEPT1) Family&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p><span>These phosphotransfer enzymes catalyze both phosphatidylcholine (PC) and 
phosphatidylethanolamine (PE) biosynthesis from CDP-choline and 
CDP-ethanolamine, respectively, plus diglycerides, in a process coupled to transmembrane
 phospholipid transport to distribute phosphatidyl choline to the 
lumenal surface of the endoplasmic reticulum (<a class='reflink' href='#ref34545206'>Henneberry <em>et al.</em> 2000</a>). </span></p>
<div>
<p>The human <span class='highlight'>CEPT1</span> uses diradylglycerols (diacylglycerols) as substrates, and the fatty acyl specificities of <span class='highlight'>CEPT1</span> for these molecules in a mixed micellar assay have been studied (<span id='ref34548666.12216837' class='cite'>Wright and McMaster 2002</span>). <span class='highlight'>CEPT1</span> displayed an apparent Km of 36 microM for CDP-<span class='highlight'>choline</span> and 4.2 mol% for di-18:<span class='highlight'>1</span> DAG with a Vmax of 14.3 nmol/min/mg. When CDP-ethanolamine was used as substrate, the apparent Km was 98 microM for CDP-ethanolamine and 4.3 mol% for di-18:<span class='highlight'>1</span> DAG with a Vmax of 8.2 nmol/min/mg. The preferred diradylglycerol substrates used by <span class='highlight'>CEPT1</span> with CDP-<span class='highlight'>choline</span> as the phosphobase donor were di-18:<span class='highlight'>1</span> DAG, di-16:<span class='highlight'>1</span> DAG, and 16:0/18:<span class='highlight'>1</span> DAG.&nbsp; When 
CDP-ethanolamine was used as the phosphobase donor, 16:0/18:<span class='highlight'>1</span> DAG, di-18:<span class='highlight'>1</span> DAG, and di-16:<span class='highlight'>1</span> DAG were the preferred substrates. The mixed micelle assay allowed
 the lipid activation of CEPT to be measured, and both the 
cholinephosphotransferase and <span class='highlight'>ethanolaminephosphotransferase</span> activities displayed the unusual property of product activation. The protein kinase C inhibitor chelerythrine and the human DAG kinase inhibitor R59949 both inhibited <span class='highlight'>CEPT1</span> activity with IC50 values of 40 microM (<span id='ref34548666.12216837' class='cite'>Wright and McMaster 2002</span>).</p>
