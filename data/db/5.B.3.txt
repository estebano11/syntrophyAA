<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='center'><span style='font-family: TIMES;'><strong> 5.B.3  The <em>Geobacter</em> Nanowire Electron Transfer (G-NET) Family </strong></span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: TIMES;'><strong> </strong> </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: TIMES;'> Bacteria have the capacity to transfer electrons from cytoplasmic electron donors to extracellular substances.  <em>Geobacter</em> and other bacteria from diverse bacterial kingdoms have this capacity.  <em>Geobacter</em> utilizes 'nanowires' distantly related to type IV pili (fimbriae) to transfer electrons to iron oxide (Fe<sub>2</sub></span>O<sub>3</sub>; rust) to generate soluble Fe<sup>2+</sup> and solid magnetite.  The entire pathway of electron transfer has been elucidated (see <span id='ref3472.16778836' class='cite'>Lovley, 2006</span>).  An electron pair is transferred from NADH to menaquinone, and then single electrons are transferred through the chain to iron oxide via an inner membrane (IM), periplasm, outer membrane (OM) pathway as follows:</p>
<p align='center'>NADH &rarr; NADHDH (3.D.1.4.1) &rarr; Menaquinone &rarr; MacA (IM) &rarr; PpcA (periplasm)</p>
<p align='center'>&rarr; OmcB (a trans OM protein) &rarr; OmcE (outer surface of the OM) &rarr;</p>
<p align='center'>OmcS (outer surface of the OM) &rarr; pilin &rarr; Fe<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub>.</p>
<p>These proteins are all c-type cytochromes (<span id='ref3472.16778836' class='cite'>Lovley, 2006</span>). Extracellular reduction of uranium via <em>Geobacter </em>conductive pili provides a protective cellular mechanism (<span id='ref8291.21896750' class='cite'>Cologgi <em>et al.</em>, 2011</span>).</p>
<p><em>Shewanella</em> nanowires display a surprising non-linear electrical transport behavior, where the voltage dependence of the conductance reveals peaks indicating discrete energy levels with higher electronic density of states (El <span id='ref5174.18441026' class='cite'>Naggar <em>et al</em>., 2008</span>). The molecular constitutes along <em>Shewanella</em> nanowires apparently possess intricate electronic structures that play roles in mediating transport.&nbsp;<span id='ref7372.20937892' class='cite'>El-Naggar <em>et al.</em> (2010)</span> have quantitated electrical transport along bacterial nanowires from <em>S. oneidensis</em> and showed that the process is absolutely dependent on two c-type decaheme cytochromes, MtrC and OmcA.</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><HR>This family belongs to the <A HREF="superfamily.php?id=89">Transmembrane One Electron Transfer Cytochrome (TM-Cyt) Superfamily</A>.<BR><HR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Cologgi, D.L., S.
