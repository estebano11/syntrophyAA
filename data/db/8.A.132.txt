<p>Interferon and interleukin receptors are thought to have evolved from a common ancestor in the early jawed vertebrates (<span id='ref34551332.28303139' class='cite'>Secombes and Zou 2017</span>). The Supressor of Lurcher 1, SOL1 (TC# 8.A.132.1.1), of 557 aas and 2 TMSs, one N-terminal 
and one C-terminal, is a homolog of these receptors. SOL1 proteins regulate ionotropic glutamate receptor
 desensitization (<span id='ref34547472.16818875' class='cite'>Walker <em>et al.</em> 2006</span>).
