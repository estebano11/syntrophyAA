<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style="text-align: center;"><strong>8.A.147.&nbsp; The Peroxiredoxin Heme-binding Protein, Tpx1 (Tpx1) Family</strong></p>
<p>Tpx1 is a peroxiredoxin that binds hemin and regulates the heme-iron/siderophore-iron transporter 3, Str3 (TC# 2.A.1.16.9). Tpx1 (Q74887; 192 aas and 0 - 2 possible TMSs), is a high affinity binding partner of Str3 (<span id="ref34552138.33140466" class="cite">Normant <em>et al.</em> 2020</span>). Under microaerobic conditions, cells deficient in heme biosynthesis and 
lacking the heme receptor Shu1 exhibit poor hemin-dependent growth in 
the absence of Tpx1, a cytoplasmic heme binding protein. Tpx1 exhibits 
an equilibrium constant value of 0.26 muM for hemin. The association of 
Tpx1 with hemin protects hemin from degradation by H<sub>2</sub>O<sub>2</sub>, and the peroxidase activity of hemin is lowered when it is bound to Tpx1 (<a class="reflink" href="http://www.tcdb.org/search/result.php?tc=2.A.1#ref34552138">Normant <em>et al.</em> 2020</a>).</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Chen, J.W., C.
