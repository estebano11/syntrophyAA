<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>8.A.172.&nbsp; The &alpha;-Crystallin Chaperone (CryA) Family&nbsp; </strong></p>
<p>Lens epithelial cells are the parental cells responsible for growth and 
development of the transparent ocular lens. Factors that initiate and 
regulate lens epithelial cell differentiation have been well characterized (<span id='ref34553389.18093866' class='cite'>Andley 2008</span>). They serve key 
transport and cell maintenance functions and are the 
primary source of metabolic activity in the lens. The molecular chaperones, the &alpha;-crystallins, are abundant 
proteins. Besides their important roles in the 
refractive and light focusing properties of the lens, alpha-crystallins 
have been implicated in a number of non-refractive pathways including 
those involving stress response, apoptosis and cell survival. Evidence for their importance in the lens epithelium resulted from studies on the properties of lens epithelial cells from alphaA
 and alphaB-crystallin gene knockout mice (<span id='ref34553389.18093866' class='cite'>Andley 2008</span>).</p>
<p>Among the transport proteins known to depend on &alpha;-crystallins are the Na<sup>+</sup> channel, Nav1.5 (<span id='ref34553388.34086898' class='cite'>Nguyen <em>et al.</em> 2021</span>), mitochondrial electron transport (<span id='ref34553391.33208022' class='cite'>Alam <em>et al.</em> 2020</span>), amyloid-beta aggregation (<span id='ref34553392.32377707' class='cite'>Ren <em>et al.</em> 2020</span>), members of the TREK‑TRAAK K2P channel family (including TREK‑1, TREK‑2 and TRAAK) (<span id='ref34553393.30106090' class='cite'>Huang <em>et al.</em> 2018</span>), the Wilson Disease copper ATPase, ATP7B, (<span id='ref34553394.29954118' class='cite'>Allocca <em>et al.</em> 2018</span>), the muscle calcium ATPase, SERCA (<span id='ref34553395.22360692' class='cite'>Dremina <em>et al.</em> 2012</span>), and many others.</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Alam, S., C.S.
