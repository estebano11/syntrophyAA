<p align='CENTER'><strong> 8.A.5  The Voltage-gated K<sup>+</sup> Channel &beta;-subunit (Kv&beta;) Family </strong></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'>Many &beta;-subunits of voltage-gated K<sup>+</sup></span> channels (VIC superfamily, TC #1.A.1.2.1-3) have been sequenced and functionally characterized.  The one from rat has been crystallized, and a 2.1 &Aring; resolution structure is available (Gulbis, 2000).  The mammalian &beta;-subunits are 350-410 residues in length. They exhibit extensive sequence similarity with many ubiquitous oxidoreductases, with bacterial stress response proteins and with plant auxin-induced proteins. These &beta;-subunits are to some extent interchangeable, and a variety of alternative splice variants are found.  They regulate various aspects of the voltage-gated mammalian channels as well as the <em>Drosophila Shaker</em> K<sup>+</sup> channel.  Thus they affect the voltage-dependence of the activation process, the rate of deactivation and both N-type and C-type inactivation.  The conserved C-terminal domain of the &beta;-subunit interacts with the conserved N-terminal hydrophilic domain of the <em>Shaker</em> &alpha;-subunit, the same region that determines the compatibility of &alpha;-subunits. The <em>hyperkinetic</em> gene of <em>Drosophila</em> encodes a &beta;-subunit of 546 amino acyl residues that when defective produces the <em>Shaker</em>-like, ether-sensitive leg shaking.</p>
<p>Regulatory &beta;-subunits of other channel proteins of the VIC superfamily (TC #1.A.1) function in distinct regulatory capacities, and some are non-homologous to members of the Kv&beta; family. Myocardial blood flow is control by oxygen-sensing vascular Kvbeta proteins (<span id='ref34552648.33499656' class='cite'>Ohanyan <em>et al.</em> 2021</span>).</p>
