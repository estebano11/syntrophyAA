<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='CENTER'><strong>8.A.8   The Phosphotransferase System HPr (HPr) Family</strong></p>
<p align='JUSTIFY'>The HPr family consists of bacterial proteins, all of which function as phosphoryl transfer proteins. They are energy-coupling constituents of the phosphotransferase system (PTS) (TC #4.A.1-4.A.7) which catalyzes sugar uptake via a group translocation mechanism. HPr proteins are not known to be homologous to any non-PTS proteins. The <em>E. coli</em> genome encodes five HPr paralogues. The functions of several of these proteins are known.
