<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p align='CENTER'><strong><span style='font-family: Times;'>9.A.4 The YggT or Fanciful K<sup>+</sup> Uptake-B (FkuB; YggT) Family </span></strong><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> </span></p>
<p><span style='font-family: Times;'> Nakamura et al. (<span id='ref3508.8982386' class='cite'>1996</span>) identified a DNA fragment from <em>Vibrio alginolyticus</em> which complemented the growth defect of a K<sup>+</sup></span> uptake deficient mutant of <em>E. coli</em> TK420.  Two of the residing genes on this fragment were reported to be required for growth in synthetic medium containing 10 mM K<sup>+</sup> and 100 mM Na<sup>+</sup>.  K<sup>+</sup> uptake was restored by introducing these genes.</p>
<p>Homologues of FkuB (most of 180-200 aas or of 80-100 aas) include the <em>E. coli</em> YggT protein.  The larger members of this family contain a repeat found in many integral membrane proteins; the smaller members have just one.</p>
<p>In the <em>E. coli</em> YggT protein (188 aas), residues 1-91 are repeated (92-182).  Half sized homologues (e.g., the YggT homologues of <em>Geobacter uraniumreducens</em>; 105 aas; gi#88936025, and <em>Bacillus anthracis</em>; YlmG; 87 aas; gi#49180675, both have just 2 TMSs).  Other homologues are much larger with more than four TMSs (e.g., from <em>Wolbachia</em>; 992 aas; 6-8 putative TMSs with a large N-terminal hydrophilic domain and a smaller C-terminal hydrophilic domain) and the Type IV secretory pathway VirB6 component of <em>Wolbachia</em> (gi#58585051; 1040 aas; 8 putative TMSs with a long hydrophilic N-terminus of &gt;600 aas).</p>
<p>The proposed transport function catalyzed by FkuB is:</p>
