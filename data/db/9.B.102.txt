<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>9.B.102 The YedE/YeeE (YeeE) Family </strong></p>
<p style='text-align: left;'>The YeeE family (also called the COG2392 or DUF395 family) consists of numerous putative transport proteins of from 4 to 10 TMSs (G Madrigal, P Kabran and M Saier, unpublished observations). Some of these (i.e PmpA and PmpB, both of 4 TMSs) may function together as a heterodimeric transporter. In some cases, two YeeE homologues are encoded in a single operon with a member of the TSUP family e.g., PmpC (2.A.102.4.2; <span id='ref7482.21183667' class='cite'>Gristwood <em>et al.</em>, 2011</span>). Some evidence suggests that some of these proteins may play a role in sulfur and/or selenium metabolism (<span id='ref34543719.25638258' class='cite'>Lin <em>et al.</em> 2015</span>). The smaller proteins (4 - 6 TMSs) have one of two conserved, related motifs, A or B, while the larger proteins (9 - 10 TMSs) have both in either of the two possible orders, A - B or B - A within two repeat segemnts of 5 TMSs (unpublished results).&nbsp;</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Bagchi, A.
