<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>9.B.157 The Cell Shape-determining MreBCD (MreBCD) Family&nbsp; </strong></p>
<p>The <em>minCD/mreBCD</em> genes together comprise an operon (<span id='ref10993.1400224' class='cite'>Levin <em>et al.</em> 1992</span>).The MreB (also called FtsA) proteins of <em>Escherichia coli</em>, <em>Bacillus <span class='highlight'>subtilis</span></em> and <em>Caulobacter crescentus</em> form actin-like cables lying beneath the 
cell surface (<span id='ref10992.14588250' class='cite'>Soufo and Graumann 2003</span>). These MreB proteins are homologous to Hsp70 chaparone proteins (TC# 1.A.33) and distantly related to exopolyphosphatases andPpx/GppA family phosphatases. The cables are required to guide longitudinal cell wall 
synthesis, and their absence leads to merodiploid spherical and inflated 
cells prone to cell lysis. In <em>B. <span class='highlight'>subtilis, E. coli</span></em> and <em>C. crescentus</em>, the <em>mreB</em> gene is essential. <em>E. coli</em> cells depleted 
of <em>mreBCD</em> became spherical, enlarged and finally lyse. Depletion of 
each <em><span class='highlight'>mre</span></em> gene 
separately conferred similar gross changes in cell morphology and 
viability. Thus, the three proteins encoded by <em>mreBCD</em> are all essential 
and function in the same morphogenetic pathway.</p>
<p>Interestingly, the 
presence of a multicopy plasmid carrying the <em>ftsQAZ</em> genes suppressed the
 lethality of deletions in the <em><span class='highlight'>mre</span></em> operon (<span id='ref10991.15612918' class='cite'>Kruse <em>et al.</em> 2005</span>). The 
MreC and MreD proteins associate with the cell membrane, and MreC interacts with both 
MreB and MreD. Thus, the <em>E. coli</em> MreBCD complex forms an essential 
membrane-bound complex. While MreB has 0 TMSs, MreC has 1 N-terminal TMS, and MreD has 5 or 6 TMSs. It has been proposed that the membrane-associated 
MreBCD complex directs longitudinal cell wall synthesis in a process 
essential to maintain cell morphology (<span id='ref10991.15612918' class='cite'>Kruse <em>et al.</em> 2005</span>).&nbsp; The<em> Bacillus halodurans</em> MreD is similar in sequence to members of ABC sub-family 3.A.1.26 in TCDB, warranting inclusion of MreBCD in TCDB.&nbsp; No direct evidence implicates MreD in transport.</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Kruse, T., J.
