<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>9.B.158 The Cut Copper Homeostasis (Cut) Family&nbsp;</strong></p>
<p>CutC is one of the six members of Cut family proteins, involved in prokaryotic copper
homeostasis. The human homolog of CutC (hCutC) is an intracellular copper-binding protein with unknown
physiological function. The Cut <span class='highlight'>protein</span> family is associated with <span class='highlight'>copper</span> <span class='highlight'>homeostasis</span> and involved in uptake, storage, delivery, and efflux of <span class='highlight'>copper</span>. <span class='highlight'>CutC</span> is a member of the Cut family and is suggested to be involved in efflux
 trafficking of cuprous ion. <span id='ref34544837.19878721' class='cite'>Li <em>et al.</em> 2010</span> reported the biochemical and 
structural characterization of human <span class='highlight'>CutC</span> (hCutC). hCutC can bind Cu<sup>+</sup> with a stoichiometry of 1:1 and an 
apparent dissociation constant of 15.5+/-2.8 microM. hCutC assumes a 
typical TIM-barrel fold and forms a tetramer in both crystal structure 
and solution which is different from the dimeric architecture of the 
bacterial <span class='highlight'>CutC</span>. Structure analysis and sequence comparison of <span class='highlight'>CutC</span> <span class='highlight'>proteins</span> from different species revealed two strictly conserved Cys residues on 
the inner surface of the C-terminal end of the TIM-barrel. Mutations of 
the two Cys residues impair the ability of 
hCutC to bind Cu<sup>+</sup>. Their results suggest that hCutC functions as an enzyme 
with Cu<sup>+</sup> as a cofactor rather than a <span class='highlight'>copper</span> transporter.&nbsp; The potential Cu<sup>+</sup>-binding site consists of the two Cys residues and other conserved residues in the vicinity.</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Gupta, S.D., B.T.
