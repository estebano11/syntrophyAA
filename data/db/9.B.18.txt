<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>9.B.18 The Xanthan Glycosyl Transferase, GumD (GumD) Family</strong></p>
<p>The GumD family cosists of a large number of glycosyltransferases involved in exo-polysaccharide biosynthesis and export.&nbsp; GumD of Xanthomonas campestris is a costituent of a multicomponent system involved in the biosynthesis and export to the cell surface.&nbsp; Some of the constituents of this system include GumB (TC# 1.B.18.3.7) and GumC (TC#8.A.3.1.3) (<span id='ref34544080.24927643' class='cite'>Bianco <em>et al.</em> 2014</span>).&nbsp; The N-terminus of GumD (residues 30 - 140) consists of a domain of closely packed 4 TMSs, followed by a hydrophilic domain that includes 1 or 2 additional TMSs.&nbsp; Residues 75 - 275 consist of the NADB_Rossmann or CoA binding_3 domain, while residues 300 to the end consist of the Bacterial Transferase (bac_transf) domain of CDD.&nbsp;</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Bianco, M.I., M.
