<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>9.B.29 The 4-5 TMS Putative Chaperone (4-5PC) Family</strong></p>
<p>The 4-5PC family consists of proteins from Spirochaetes (<em>Leptospira</em> (subfamily 1)) and many potentially distant homologues in other bacteria (subfamilies 2 - 4) as well as archaea (subfamily 5).&nbsp; They have 200 - 270 aas with 4 - 5 TMSs. Some of them are encoded adjacent to genes coding for membrane fusion proteins (MFPs; TC# 8.A.1) and outer menbrane factors (OMFs; TC# 1.B.17) as well as ABC transporters (<span id='ref34549976.22707703' class='cite'>Yamada <em>et al.</em> 2012</span>).&nbsp; They may be important for assembly of multicomponent transporters such as ABC systems (<span id='ref34549976.22707703' class='cite'>Yamada <em>et al.</em> 2012</span>). These proteins may be distantly related to members of the ABC superfamily (TC# 3.A.1), as well as members of the golgi Yip1 family (TC# 9.B.135) and the UAB5 family (TC# 9.B.333), but these suggestions have not been confirmed.&nbsp;</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><HR>This family belongs to the <A HREF="superfamily.php?id=102">YIP Superfamily</A>.<BR><HR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Drew, D., D.
