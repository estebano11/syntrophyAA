<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>&nbsp;</strong><span style='font-weight: bold;'>9.B.36 The Acid Resistance Membrane Protein (HdeD) Family</span></p>
<p>The HdeD protein of <em>E. coli</em> is a 190 aa protein required for induction of acid resistance in response to overexpression of the AraC-type regulator, YdeO (<a class='reflink' href='#ref3512'>Masuda and Church, 2003</a>).  It has 6 TMSs in a 2 +   2   + 2                                                                                                           arrangement.  Evidence for an internally triplicated 2 TMS element was found.  This protein may function with a 194 aa, 2 TMS protein (gi #3288672).</p>
<p>Homologues of HdeD include the DR3 protein of the moss, <em>Tortula rutalis</em> (223 aas; AAN87348) which also has 6 putative TMSs.  Other homologues are annotated 'putative permease.'  Homologues are generally of 160-240 aas in length with 6 TMSs in a 2   + 2 +   2 or 3 + 3 TMS arrangement, where the latter proteins show the first 3 TMSs are separated by larger hydrophilic loops than the last 3 TMSs.  They derive from proteobacteria, cyanobacteria, plants, low and high G C Gram-positive bacteria and archaea.  Some are much bigger (&gt;450 aas) due to the presence of large hydrophilic extensions.&nbsp; This is a large superfamily with over 10 families (see entries under 9.B.36).&nbsp; These proteins are known as the DUF4386 domain proteins.</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Banesh, S. and V.
