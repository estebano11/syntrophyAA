<TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="description"></a>&nbsp;<p><p style='text-align: center;'><strong>9.B.98 The DUF95 (DUF95) Family</strong></p>
<p style='text-align: left;'>The DUF95 family consists of proteins that exhibit 4, 5 or 6 TMSs. They usually contain about 200 aas but can have N-terminal hydrophilic extensions of &gt;100 aas. Members are found in archaea and bacteria. DUF95 domains are found in a few ABC transport systems such as 3.A.1.156.1 where the system may have an extra 4 TMS subunit.&nbsp; Another ABC transport system having this domain is the 18 TMS protein (3.A.1.144.4) which has a 6 + 6 + 6 arrangement, and the last 6 TMSs correspond to the DUF95 domain.&nbsp;&nbsp; It has been suggested that DUF95 proteins are auxilary subunits of bacteriocin ABC exporters (<a class='reflink' href='#ref7893'>van Belkum <em>et al.</em>, 2011</a>). These proteins may be distantly related to TatC (2.A.64).</p><BR></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5><a name="references"></a>&nbsp;<p><B>References:</B><P></TD></TR><TR><TD COLSPAN=5>Chastanet, A. and R.
